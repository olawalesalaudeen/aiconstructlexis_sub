## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, BookSum is consistently described as a benchmark dataset used to evaluate summarization capabilities. The definitions specify its focus on generating "summaries of entire books" and assessing performance on "long input texts." Papers use this instrument to measure the behavior of Text summarization. One study further operationalizes its use as a way "to assess the long-context and long-response generation capabilities" of models. The available data shows a uniform understanding of BookSum as a tool for evaluating how models handle summarization tasks involving book-length documents.
