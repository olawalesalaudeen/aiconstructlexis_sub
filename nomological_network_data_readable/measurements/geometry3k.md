# Geometry3k
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  
**Also known as:** Geometry3K  

## State of the Field

Geometry3k is characterized in the provided literature as both a benchmark and a dataset for evaluating reasoning in the domain of plane geometry. The prevailing usage is as an instrument to measure mathematical reasoning and geometry problem solving. One definition describes it as an "early multimodal reasoning benchmark," while another provides more detail on its composition, defining it as a dataset with "human-annotated logical forms." These logical forms are reportedly used to generate "factual QA pairs" for model evaluation. The data shows that Geometry3k is often used in conjunction with other geometry benchmarks like GeoQA, UniGeo, and GEOS. Furthermore, it is sometimes incorporated into larger evaluation suites, as one paper notes that a split of the MathVista benchmark is "collected from... Geometry3K."
