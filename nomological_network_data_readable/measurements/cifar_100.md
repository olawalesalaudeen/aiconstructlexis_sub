**Type:** Measurement

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, CIFAR-100 is consistently characterized as an image dataset with 100 object classes used for evaluation. Its most prevalent application is to measure and benchmark model performance on the task of `image classification`. Several papers explicitly frame it as a more complex or challenging vision task compared to CIFAR-10, sometimes describing its 100 classes as "fine-grained". While its primary use is for standard image classification, a smaller set of studies employs it to assess more specific behaviors. For instance, some papers use it to evaluate `generalization`, particularly for prompt-based methods, while others use it for `zero-shot image classification` experiments. The dataset also serves as a testbed for a variety of other evaluations, including text-conditioned clustering, automated accuracy prediction under data shifts, neural compression techniques, and `memory efficiency`.
