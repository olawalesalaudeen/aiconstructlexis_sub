# PHEME
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

PHEME is presented in the provided literature as a benchmark dataset designed for fake news detection. The single available definition characterizes it as a collection of tweets associated with five major news events. Its primary application, as indicated by its relationships, is to measure the behavior of misinformation detection. One study describes it as a "widely-used" benchmark, evaluating it alongside other datasets like Twitter and Weibo ("HALoGEN: FantasticLLMHallucinations and Where to Find Them"). This same paper provides specific details on its composition, noting that PHEME consists of 1,414 training tweets and 608 testing tweets.
