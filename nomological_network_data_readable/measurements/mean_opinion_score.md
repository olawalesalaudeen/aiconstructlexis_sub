# Mean Opinion Score
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  
**Also known as:** MOS  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, Mean Opinion Score (MOS) is consistently framed as a subjective evaluation metric that relies on human raters. Its application, however, varies by modality. One paper defines and uses MOS to assess the quality of synthesized speech, where human listeners rate attributes such as "naturalness" on a 1–5 scale. In contrast, another study applies MOS to evaluate text, using a "Likert-like scale" for human judgments on "text quality" and "diversity." In the context of text evaluation, the qualities it is said to capture include perceived fluency and coherence. Therefore, while the core mechanism of human-rated scoring is shared, the specific phenomena measured by MOS—speech naturalness versus text quality and diversity—differ depending on the research context.
