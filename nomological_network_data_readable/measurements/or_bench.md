# OR-Bench
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

OR-Bench is presented in the provided literature as a large-scale measurement instrument designed to assess refusal behaviors in large language models. According to its introductory paper, the benchmark's purpose is to measure the "over-refusal tendency of models" by using 80,000 safe prompts across 10 categories that are likely to be incorrectly rejected. The same paper frames it as the "first large-scale over-refusal benchmark." Across the surveyed work, OR-Bench is consistently used as an evaluation tool. It is most commonly cited as a measurement for the general behavior of "Refusal to answer," and is also used to specifically measure the construct of "Overrefusal."
