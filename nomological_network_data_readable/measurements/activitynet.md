# ActivityNet
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided sources, ActivityNet is consistently characterized as a dataset used for evaluation in vision-language research. It is defined as a large-scale collection of diverse web videos for tasks related to "activity understanding" or "video understanding." The most common application mentioned is for generation tasks, with sources citing its use for "activity generation" and "conditional video generation." One definition specifically situates it as the basis for the activity generation task within the InterleavedBench evaluation benchmark. As a measurement instrument, ActivityNet is used to assess specific capabilities, with papers in this set using it to measure both `Planning` and `Action recognition`. One study further illustrates its use in validating model capabilities in "V2P generation, text-based video content editing, and video generation" ("Pun Unintended:LLMs and the Illusion of Humor Understanding").
