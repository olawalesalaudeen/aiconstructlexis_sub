# HELM
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 22 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, HELM is predominantly characterized as a large-scale, comprehensive benchmark for language models, with its full name being Holistic Evaluation of Language Models. Most sources describe it as a framework for evaluating models across a wide range of scenarios, metrics, capabilities, limitations, and risks. It is frequently listed alongside other evaluation suites such as MMLU, BigBench, and Open LLM Leaderboards. In terms of its application, one paper uses HELM to rank the performance of LLMs on the MMLU benchmark, and it is generally positioned as a "proxy measurement of the true capabilities of LLMs." A single paper presents a starkly different definition, describing HELM as a non-adaptive attack method for watermark evasion that involves adding typos or converting text to lowercase. The benchmark is also reported to be "strongly correlated" with LiveBench.
