# LegalBench
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  
**Also known as:** Legal-bench  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, LegalBench is consistently characterized as a measurement instrument for evaluating language models in the legal domain. The prevailing usage, as described in multiple definitions and relationships, is to assess the construct of legal reasoning. One paper specifies its composition, noting that "LegalBench consists of 162 tasks that evaluate various aspects of legal reasoning" ("TracSum: A New Benchmark for Aspect-Based Summarization with Sentence-Level Traceability in Medical Domain"). A less frequent framing also describes it as a dataset for evaluating hallucination and domain-specific factual accuracy in legal contexts. In practice, researchers use the benchmark for "cross-evaluations" and to "test models," operationalizing the assessment with metrics like balanced accuracy.
