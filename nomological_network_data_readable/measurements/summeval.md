# SummEval
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 11 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, SummEval is consistently characterized as a benchmark dataset used to evaluate text summarization. Its primary stated purpose is to assess text summary quality, which one paper frames more broadly as the evaluation of text in terms of "high-level concepts." The operationalization of this measurement relies on human evaluation, with one source specifying that annotations are provided by three human raters. A prevalent framing across multiple papers is that these human judgments are structured along four specific dimensions: fluency, coherence, consistency, and relevance. This evaluation is captured using Likert-scale scores, which one paper details as ranging from 1 to 5. In line with this design, the dataset is explicitly used to perform "text summarization evaluation."
