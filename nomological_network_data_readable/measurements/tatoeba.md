# Tatoeba
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Within the provided literature, Tatoeba is characterized as a measurement instrument used for cross-lingual sentence retrieval. It is specifically defined as the validation split of a dataset, and its described purpose is to serve as a tool for the independent validation of research findings. The single available source presents its use in conjunction with other datasets, such as BUCC-18, to confirm results. As one paper notes, its authors "present results on the validation split of Tatoeba... for an independent validation of our findings" (Inference Compute-Optimal Video Vision Language Models).
