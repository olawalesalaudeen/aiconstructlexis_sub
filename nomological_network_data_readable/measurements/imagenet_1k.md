## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, ImageNet-1k is predominantly characterized as a large-scale benchmark dataset used to measure `Image classification`. Most definitions describe it as containing over a million images organized into 1,000 object categories, and its most frequent application is for evaluating core classification accuracy, though it is also widely used for pre-training vision models. A smaller but notable line of work employs ImageNet-1k to evaluate `Image generation`, with one paper calling it a "gold standard benchmark" for this task and another specifying the use of FID and Inception Score for assessing quality. Beyond these uses, the dataset is also operationalized to measure a range of other behaviors, including `Zero-shot image classification`, `Multimodal alignment`, and `Object recognition`. The data shows varied applications, from using its validation set to construct typographic attacks to modifying its images into flattened patches to evaluate cross-modal compression. Some studies also use it to assess `Generalization` and `Multimodal understanding`. The provided data also defines related but distinct benchmarks, such as `ImageNet-A`, which is specifically used to evaluate model robustness against natural adversarial examples.
