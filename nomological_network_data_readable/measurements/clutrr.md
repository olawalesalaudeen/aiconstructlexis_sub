## State of the Field

In the provided literature, CLUTRR is characterized as a benchmark for evaluating relation inference. Its core task requires a model to deduce "familial relations from partial graph descriptions in natural language," as stated in one source. This benchmark is used to operationalize and measure specific reasoning abilities. For instance, one paper uses CLUTRR as a measure for both Logical reasoning and Complex reasoning. The consistent framing across the available sources positions CLUTRR as an instrument for assessing a model's capacity to infer relationships from textual descriptions of a graph.
