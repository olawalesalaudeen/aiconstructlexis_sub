## State of the Field

CharXiv is consistently described across the provided literature as a benchmark dataset for evaluating model capabilities related to charts and graphs. The benchmark is explicitly used to measure "Chart and diagram understanding" and "Commonsense knowledge." Its primary function, as stated in multiple sources, is to evaluate "complex visual reasoning through the interpretation of charts and graphs" across various scientific and technical domains. A defining feature of this instrument is its operationalization through "real-world chart data sourced from arXiv preprints," which is noted to expand the scope of chart understanding scenarios. For instance, one study uses the CharXiv validation set to highlight model performance on tasks involving "complex visual reasoning and interpretation of charts and graphs" (FR-Spec: Accelerating Large-Vocabulary Language Models via Frequency-Ranked Speculative Sampling). The available sources present a unified view of CharXiv as an evaluation tool for these specific reasoning abilities.
