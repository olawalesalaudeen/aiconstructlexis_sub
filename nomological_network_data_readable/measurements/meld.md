# MELD
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, MELD is consistently described as a multimodal dataset for evaluating emotion recognition in conversational speech. It is defined as a multi-party dataset composed of dialogues from the TV series 'Friends', containing, according to one source, over 1,400 dialogues and 13,000 utterances. These dialogues are annotated with both emotion and sentiment labels, with one paper specifying seven emotion categories. The prevailing use of MELD is to operationalize and measure classification tasks, specifically 'Speech Emotion Recognition (SER)' and 'Emotion Detection'. As one paper frames it, MELD is an "emotion classification benchmark with multimodal conversational utterances" ('Crowdsource, Crawl, or Generate?...'). While MELD is also listed as a measurement for 'Emotion understanding' and 'Faithfulness', the provided data does not include evidence quotes to elaborate on these applications, making its use for emotion classification the most substantiated role.
