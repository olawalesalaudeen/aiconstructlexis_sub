# LaMP
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, LaMP is consistently described as a public benchmark designed to evaluate LLM personalization. The benchmark is specifically positioned to assess short-form personalized text generation, with one paper noting it tests "surface-level personalization on short-context queries" ('Case-Based Decision-Theoretic Decoding with Quality Memories'). This focus on brevity is reinforced in another study that contrasts LaMP with the LongLaMP benchmark, which is used for "long-form personalized text generation" ('SongComposer: A Large Language Model for Lyric and Melody Generation in Song Composition'). The tasks included within the LaMP benchmark are varied, with sources collectively mentioning news headline generation, scholarly title generation, tweet paraphrasing, citation identification, movie tagging, and Amazon product rating. One paper explicitly enumerates these as distinct, numbered components, such as LaMP-1 for citation identification and LaMP-4 for news headline generation.
