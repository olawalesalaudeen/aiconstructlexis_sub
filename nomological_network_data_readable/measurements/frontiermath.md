# FrontierMath
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the available literature, FrontierMath is a benchmark designed to evaluate the complex problem-solving and reasoning abilities of Large Language Models. The paper "RE-IMAGINE: Symbolic Benchmark Synthesis for Reasoning Evaluation" puts forward this definition, citing it as a "reasoning benchmark" where models have achieved "unprecedented results." While its primary stated purpose is to assess reasoning, other work uses it to operationalize different constructs. Specifically, the paper "Position: Don’t Use the CLT in LLM Evals With Fewer Than a Few Hundred Datapoints" employs FrontierMath as a measurement for Faithfulness. Thus, within this set of papers, FrontierMath is framed both as an evaluator of reasoning and as an instrument for measuring faithfulness.
