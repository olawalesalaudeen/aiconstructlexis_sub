# MathVerse
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 11 papers  
**Also known as:** MATHVERSE  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, MathVerse is consistently described as a multimodal benchmark used to assess mathematical reasoning in vision-language models, with a recurring focus on geometry-related problems. The instrument is primarily used to measure the construct of "Mathematical reasoning," and in one case, model "Generalization." Operationally, MathVerse is composed of multiple-choice questions, and one paper specifies that it presents questions in text-dominant, vision-integrated, and vision-only formats. It is frequently evaluated alongside other visual math benchmarks like MathVista, MathVision, and DynaMath to gauge model capabilities and demonstrate what one source calls the "current limitations of VLMs." A minority of sources offer specific characterizations, with one describing it as a "static" benchmark and another noting that it exhibits "high redundancy" and a "strong correlation with other benchmarks".
