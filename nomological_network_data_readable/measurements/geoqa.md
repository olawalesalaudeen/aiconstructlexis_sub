# GeoQA
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  
**Also known as:** GeoQA+  

## State of the Field

GeoQA is consistently characterized as a question-answering benchmark used to evaluate multimodal geometric problem solving from questions and images. Across the provided literature, it serves as an instrument to measure mathematical reasoning, with papers using it to assess performance on geometry problem-solving tasks. The benchmark is frequently employed alongside a suite of other mathematical and geometry-focused datasets, such as MathVista, UniGeo, Geometry3K, and MathVerse, to conduct model evaluations. Its application includes comparing new models against what are termed "conventional SOTA methods in geometry problem solving domain." An enhanced version, GeoQA+, is also described, with its training set being used for generating alignment and instruction data in one instance. One paper notes that GeoQA+ was considered the "previous largest open-source dataset" before being surpassed, indicating its scale within the field as represented in this data.
