# TinyStories
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 10 papers  
**Also known as:** Tiny Stories  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, TinyStories is consistently described as a dataset of synthetic, short, and simple stories generated by large language models, with some sources specifying GPT-3.5 and GPT-4 as the generators. Its most common stated purpose is to facilitate the training and analysis of language models, with one paper noting it "aims to represent key challenges in text generation while keeping training tractable for small to medium scale models" (The Belief State Transformer). The dataset is primarily used to measure or evaluate the capabilities of `Text generation` and `Language modeling`. Beyond these general applications, some work uses TinyStories for more specific probes, such as examining "character choices" in LLMs (Emergent Response Planning in LLMs). In practice, it is sometimes combined with other corpora, like "Children Stories," for training models on language generation tasks. The definitions are largely in agreement, emphasizing the dataset's synthetic origin and its design for tractable yet challenging model training.
