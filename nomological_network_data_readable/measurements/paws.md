# PAWS
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, PAWS is predominantly used as a benchmark to measure `Paraphrase identification`. It is defined as a "paraphrase detection benchmark" that evaluates whether two sentences are semantically equivalent, specifically assessing a model's performance in distinguishing paraphrases from non-paraphrases. While its most common application is for paraphrase-related tasks, one paper also uses PAWS to measure `Safety`. The benchmark serves as a downstream dataset for quantitative evaluation, with one study reporting "particularly notable gains of 3.17% and 12.38% in PAWS" to demonstrate the effectiveness of its methods against a conventional approach.
