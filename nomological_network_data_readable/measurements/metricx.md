# MetricX
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  

## State of the Field

MetricX is consistently characterized across the provided literature as an automatic metric for machine translation (MT) evaluation. Its primary application is to measure 'Translation quality', and it is also used as a baseline for score prediction. The provided sources describe it as a 'state-of-the-art' metric, with one paper noting it was the 'winner of the WMT’24 Metrics Shared Task' ("From Jack of All Trades to Master of One: Specializing LLM-based Autoraters to a Test Set"). While its status as a top-performing metric is a recurring theme, a less common description focuses on its architecture. One paper specifies that MetricX is 'built on mT5' and 'adopts a regression-based framework' to predict translation quality by learning from human judgments ("CAFE: Retrieval Head-based Coarse-to-Fine Information Seeking to Enhance Multi-DocumentQACapability").
