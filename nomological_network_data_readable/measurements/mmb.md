# MMB
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, the Multi-modality Benchmark (MMB) is consistently defined as a benchmark for evaluating Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs). It is characterized as providing a "robust and holistic evaluation" of these models. The primary behavior MMB is used to measure is Visual Question Answering (VQA), with one paper explicitly including it in a list of "conventional VQA tasks". In practice, MMB is frequently employed as part of a larger suite of evaluation tools. For instance, studies report using MMB alongside other widely-used MLLM benchmarks such as MME, MMMU, MathVista, and ScienceQA. This pattern of co-occurrence suggests its role as a common instrument within a broader set of evaluations for MLLMs.
