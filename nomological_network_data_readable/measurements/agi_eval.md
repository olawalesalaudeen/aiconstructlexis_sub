# Agi-Eval
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 15 papers  
**Also known as:** AgiEval, AGIEval, AGIeval, AGIEVAL, AGI Eval  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, Agi-Eval is most commonly characterized as a benchmark for evaluating the general abilities of foundation models using human-centric standardized exams. The tasks are derived from a variety of high-stakes tests, with papers citing examples such as the Gaokao (Chinese college entrance exam), American law school admission tests, SAT, LSAT, and GRE, covering subjects like English, Math, Law, and Logic. The stated purpose of the benchmark is frequently to assess "general model performance" or "overall model capabilities," with one paper specifying it measures "reasoning capabilities." In line with this, relationship data indicates that Agi-Eval is used to operationalize and measure the constructs of `Generalization` and `Commonsense knowledge`. A smaller number of sources offer alternative framings, describing it as an "aggregated benchmark dataset" for a wide range of tasks or as a "bilingual benchmark." One paper also references its methodology as an "inspiration for the paper's question-collection approach," while another broadens its scope, stating it is "designed to assess the capabilities of models in artificial general intelligence across a broad range of tasks and skills."
