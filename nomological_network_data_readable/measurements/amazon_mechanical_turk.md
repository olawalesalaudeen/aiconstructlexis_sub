# Amazon Mechanical Turk
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided sources, Amazon Mechanical Turk is consistently described as a crowdsourcing platform for obtaining human judgments. Its most common application is conducting human evaluations of model-generated outputs, such as plans and diagrams. In these evaluations, annotators are asked to provide subjective ratings or compare different model outputs on specific criteria like "fluency and descriptiveness," as noted in one study. For example, one paper used the platform to have "sighted human annotators to compare the outputs of our multimodal system with two variants of ClipCap" ("Alt-Text with Context: Improving Accessibility for Images on Twitter"). A less frequently documented use is for data collection to train models. One paper reports using the platform to "collect 13K human annotations of valid vs. invalid samples" for the purpose of training critic models ("PlaSma: Procedural Knowledge Models for Language-based Planning and Re-Planning"). Thus, the platform is operationalized as a means to gather human annotations, either for direct evaluation or as training data.
