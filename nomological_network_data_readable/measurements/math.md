## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, MATH is a prevalent benchmark used to evaluate advanced mathematical reasoning in large language models. It is consistently defined as a dataset of challenging, competition-level high school mathematics problems, frequently citing sources like the American Mathematics Competitions (AMC) and the American Invitational Mathematics Examination (AIME). The dataset is described as containing 12,500 problems spanning subjects such as algebra, geometry, and number theory, often categorized by difficulty and including step-by-step solutions with boxed answers to facilitate automated evaluation. The overwhelming majority of papers use MATH to measure `Mathematical reasoning`, often alongside GSM8K, with MATH being positioned as more difficult; as one paper states, it is "significantly more challenging than GSM8K". A smaller body of work also employs MATH to assess other constructs, including `Generalization`, specifically for "easy-to-hard generalization," as well as `Complex reasoning` and `In-context learning`. While most commonly referred to as MATH, it is sometimes called "Hendrycks-MATH," and researchers have created modified versions like "MATH*" for specific experiments or integrated it into composite datasets such as "MATH-Combined". It is also distinguished from a different benchmark named the "Mathematics Dataset" from DeepMind. Evaluation is typically performed using metrics like exact match or Pass@1 on the final answer.
