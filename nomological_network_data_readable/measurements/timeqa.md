# TimeQA
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  
**Also known as:** TIMEQA  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, TimeQA is consistently described as a benchmark for temporal or time-sensitive question answering. It is defined as being derived from natural language and Wikidata, with a focus on "real-world factual questions about event-time relationships" (Binary Classifier Optimization for Large Language Model Alignment). The instrument is used to measure constructs such as `Factuality` and `Mathematical reasoning`. Specifically, its tasks are reported to involve "ordering, arithmetic and co-temporal inference," where it reveals "large gaps between model and human performance" (FANS: Formal Answer Selection forLLMNatural Language Math Reasoning Using Lean4). One paper characterizes it as one of the larger benchmarks for this purpose. It is also used as a source for other evaluation datasets, such as DATEAUGBENCH.
