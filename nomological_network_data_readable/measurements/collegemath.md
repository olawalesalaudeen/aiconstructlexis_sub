# CollegeMath
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 8 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided sources, CollegeMath is most commonly characterized as a benchmark for assessing mathematical reasoning and problem-solving at the college level. Its prevailing use is for "out-of-domain (OOD) evaluation" to test a model's ability to generalize beyond its training data. The benchmark is described as "challenging" and is said to assess "advanced topics beyond high school curriculum." A different framing, present in one paper, describes CollegeMath as a "mathematical reasoning dataset" rather than just an evaluation tool. This source specifies that the dataset was "created using MathScale" and contains "two million math question-answer pairs." Despite this alternative description, its primary role in the surveyed literature is as an instrument for evaluating advanced mathematical capabilities.
