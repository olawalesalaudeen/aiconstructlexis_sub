**Type:** Measurement

## State of the Field

Based on the provided data, a Process Reward Model (PRM) is consistently framed as a trained model for evaluating the quality of reasoning processes. It is defined as a tool that assesses the "correctness of individual reasoning steps in a solution trajectory" to provide fine-grained feedback for agent improvement. This approach is described as facilitating a "fine-grained quality assessment of reasoning." The model is specifically utilized to "assess the step-wise accuracy of a solution S." Across the available sources, its primary application is the measurement of "Reasoning quality."
