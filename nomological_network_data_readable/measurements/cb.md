# CB
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 14 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, CB is consistently characterized as a benchmark for evaluating natural language inference (NLI). The most prevalent definition describes it as a "textual entailment benchmark" for "short premise-hypothesis pairs," and it is frequently identified as a component of the SuperGLUE benchmark. Papers use CB to measure the behavior of `Textual entailment`. A more specific framing, present in several sources, defines the task as evaluating whether a statement "commits the author to a particular belief" or a certain entailment, with outputs classified as entailment, contradiction, or neutrality. While the focus is almost always on NLI, one paper presents a broader view, categorizing CB as a "general NLU task" alongside TREC and Subj. In evaluations, CB is often grouped with other NLI and SuperGLUE tasks, such as RTE, WiC, BoolQ, and COPA, with one source noting it is "very similar to RTE".
