# CLINC150
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, CLINC150 is consistently characterized as a benchmark dataset for intent classification. It is defined as an English-language dataset containing 150 distinct intent categories. While its primary use is for classification, one paper also describes it as a benchmark for "intent clustering" and positions it as an existing benchmark for comparison purposes. Researchers use CLINC150 to operationalize and measure specific model capabilities. Most directly, it is used to evaluate a model's "Intention recognition," which aligns with its core definition. In at least one case, it is also employed as a measure of "Commonsense knowledge."
