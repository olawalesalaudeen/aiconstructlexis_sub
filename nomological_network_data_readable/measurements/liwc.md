# LIWC
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided data, LIWC (Linguistic Inquiry and Word Count) is characterized as a psycholinguistic feature extraction tool. The single available definition states that it operates by categorizing words into psychological and linguistic dimensions, with examples including emotion, cognition, and formality. One source snippet reinforces this, noting that the tool "provides psycholinguistic characteristics." In terms of its application, the data shows that LIWC is used to measure the construct of "Stylistic variation." It is also mentioned as a tool for generating features for segment comparison, sometimes alongside other instruments like WritePrint.
