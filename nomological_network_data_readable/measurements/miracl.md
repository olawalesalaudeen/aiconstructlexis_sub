# MIRACL
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, MIRACL is consistently characterized as a dataset for evaluating retrieval systems, with a specific focus on multilingual contexts. It is identified as the "Multilingual Information Retrieval Across a Continuum of Languages" dataset, designed for evaluation across 18 diverse languages. Papers use MIRACL to measure both general "Information retrieval" and, more specifically, "Multilingual ability." One study explicitly states its purpose is "to assess... multilingual retrieval capability." The dataset is described as containing "native questions" and "relevance judgements of retrieved passages" to facilitate this evaluation. In addition to direct use, MIRACL also serves as a foundation for new evaluation tools, as one paper notes its own benchmark "builds on the popular MIRACL dataset."
