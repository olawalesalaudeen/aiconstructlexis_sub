# ScanQA
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  

## State of the Field

ScanQA is consistently characterized across the provided literature as a benchmark dataset for question answering in 3D scenes. The single available definition specifies that its questions are grounded in objects found within scans from the ScanNet dataset. Its application in the surveyed papers is to measure a range of related capabilities. It is most frequently positioned as an instrument for evaluating 3D-specific phenomena, including 3D understanding, 3D perception, 3D reasoning, and the direct task of 3D question answering. Beyond this 3D focus, it is also used to assess broader abilities like vision-language reasoning and visual question answering. One paper provides a concrete example of its use, employing ScanQA to "evaluate the effectiveness of... a situated spatial alignment module on general scene understanding tasks" ("SPARTUN3D: Situated Spatial Understanding of 3D World in Large Language Model").
