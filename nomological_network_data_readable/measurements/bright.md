# BRIGHT
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, BRIGHT is consistently characterized as a benchmark for evaluating a specific type of information retrieval. Its stated purpose is to measure what papers term "reasoning-intensive retrieval" or the "reasoning implied in retrieval." The benchmark is designed to assess the ability of retrieval models to handle complex queries that, as one paper notes, "require the retriever to engage in deep reasoning to identify useful passages." A recurring point in its description is that this evaluation goes "beyond simple semantic relevance," distinguishing it from other retrieval tasks. Papers report using BRIGHT as a "challenging" instrument to evaluate the performance of various models on this specific reasoning capability. The provided sources are in complete agreement on its function and scope, with no alternative definitions or uses mentioned.
