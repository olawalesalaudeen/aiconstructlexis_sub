# GPTZero
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 12 papers  
**Also known as:** GPT-Zero  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, GPTZero is consistently characterized as a commercial service used to detect machine-generated text. It is frequently employed as a baseline or benchmark in papers that propose new detection methods. The prevailing classification for this tool is as a supervised or training-based detector, with one paper specifying that it is "trained to assess perplexity and burstiness in text" to distinguish between human and AI-generated content ("DNA-GPT: Divergent N-Gram Analysis for Training-Free Detection of GPT-Generated Text"). Several sources describe it as an API-based service, and one notes that it is a paid tool. The provided literature also includes performance evaluations, with some studies reporting that it "struggles significantly" on certain tasks or achieves low recall.
