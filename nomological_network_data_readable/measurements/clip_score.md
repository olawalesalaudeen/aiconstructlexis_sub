# CLIP score
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 8 papers  
**Also known as:** CLIPScore, CLIP Score, CLIP-Score  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, CLIP score is predominantly characterized as a reference-free metric for evaluating the alignment between text prompts and generated visual content. This evaluation is consistently operationalized by computing the cosine similarity between the CLIP embeddings of a text and a corresponding image, a method used to assess concepts like "semantic consistency" and "prompt faithfulness." Based on its application in the source papers, CLIP score is used to measure `Multimodal alignment`, `Image generation`, and `Image captioning`, with one paper also linking it to `Factuality`. One study notes its wide use "to evaluate the alignment of samples generated by text-to-image/video (T2I/V) and image captioning models" (PAK-UCB Contextual Bandit...). While the primary application is 2D image-text alignment, some work extends its use to measure the "multi-view consistency" of 3D content by averaging scores across multiple rendered views. A distinct application frames it as a method for generating rewards in reinforcement learning tasks. Despite being described as a "popular" metric, this view is not universal, as one paper characterizes it as a "simple (and supposedly worse) feature-based metric" (Who Evaluates the Evaluations?...).
