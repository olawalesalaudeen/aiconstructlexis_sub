# FLASK
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, FLASK is consistently defined as a benchmark for the fine-grained evaluation of particular large language model skill sets. One paper specifies its composition as 200 test prompts and 2000 responses generated by models such as Alpaca-7B, Vicuna-13B, Bard, and GPT-3.5-Turbo-0613. The primary application of FLASK is to measure a range of constructs, including Faithfulness, Consistency, Insightfulness, Completeness, Self-reflection, Safety, and general Output quality. One study highlights its utility for granular assessment, noting that it "offers a more granular evaluation with 12 skill-specific scores" and is used to show improvements in areas like "robustness, correctness, insightfulness," as well as "factuality, completeness, and metacognition." Beyond direct model evaluation, FLASK is also used to assess LLM-as-a-judge performance across its diverse set of model outputs. The benchmark is also studied in relation to other evaluation instruments like MMLU and GSM8k.
