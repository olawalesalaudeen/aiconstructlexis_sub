# ClothoAQA
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

ClothoAQA is consistently described across the provided sources as a benchmark or dataset for audio question answering. Its primary purpose is to evaluate a model's audio-language understanding, or as one paper frames it, the "natural language understanding of general audio content" (CalligraphicOCRforChinese Calligraphy Recognition). The dataset is derived from the Clotho dataset, with one source specifying its composition as 1,991 audio samples, "each paired with six crowdsourced questions and corresponding answers." The relationship data confirms its direct use as a measurement instrument for the behavior of audio question answering. One paper refers to it as a "foundational benchmark," and snippets suggest that accuracy (ACC) is a common evaluation metric, with a specific setting or version called "unanimous" also being noted.
