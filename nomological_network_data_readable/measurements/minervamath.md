## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, MinervaMATH is consistently treated as a benchmark dataset for evaluating mathematical reasoning. It is specifically used to assess what one paper calls "advanced quantitative reasoning" through a collection of "undergraduate- or Olympiad-level mathematical problems" ("AROMA: Autonomous Rank-one Matrix Adaptation"). The available sources all position it as an instrument to evaluate models' "mathematical reasoning capabilities." It is frequently listed alongside other datasets like GSM8K, MATH500, and AIME as part of a suite of established benchmarks for this purpose. The content of MinervaMATH is characterized as representing a high level of difficulty among mathematical reasoning datasets.
