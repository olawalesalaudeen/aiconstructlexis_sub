# AMC 2023
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 12 papers  
**Also known as:** AMC2023, AMC23  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, AMC 2023 is consistently used as a measurement instrument to evaluate mathematical reasoning in language models. It is variously referred to as a "benchmark," "dataset," or "exam," with all definitions agreeing that it consists of problems from the 2023 American Mathematics Competitions. The problems are characterized as being at the high school level and are frequently described as "challenging" or "competition-level." As one paper notes, newer datasets like it "represent higher levels of difficulty." The primary construct it is used to assess is mathematical reasoning, with some papers specifying "advanced arithmetic reasoning" or "competition-level mathematical reasoning." In evaluation settings, AMC 2023 is frequently used alongside other mathematical benchmarks such as AIME24, MATH-500, and Olympiad Bench to assess model performance on difficult problem-solving tasks.
