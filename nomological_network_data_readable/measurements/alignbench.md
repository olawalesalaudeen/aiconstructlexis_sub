# AlignBench
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, AlignBench is consistently described as a benchmark for evaluating the alignment capabilities of Large Language Models, specifically in Chinese. The evaluation methodology is a frequently mentioned feature, characterized as a "rule-calibrated, multidimensional LLM-as-Judge approach." One paper adds that this procedure incorporates Chain-of-Thought reasoning for what it calls "reliable and interpretable evaluations." While its general purpose is to assess "LLM alignment," papers also use it to measure more specific constructs; for instance, it is explicitly used to measure "Commonsense knowledge." Another paper notes that specific metrics for quality assessment, namely "Clarity, Completeness, and Factuality," are drawn from AlignBench. The benchmark itself consists of 683 samples. While most sources refer to it as a benchmark, one paper frames it as an "evaluation approach" that uses "manually set different evaluation dimensions for various types of questions."
