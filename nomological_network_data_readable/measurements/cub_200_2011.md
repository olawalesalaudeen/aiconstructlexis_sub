## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, CUB-200-2011 (also referred to as CUB, CUB-200, or Caltech-UCSD Birds-200-2011) is consistently defined as a fine-grained image dataset containing 200 species of birds. Its most prevalent application is to measure model performance on fine-grained visual classification (FGVC) or broader image classification tasks. Several papers situate it within the FGVC benchmark suite, where it is used alongside other specialized datasets. The dataset is used to evaluate a range of behaviors, including zero-shot classification, prediction correctness, and the performance of concept-based models. While the dominant use is for classification, a smaller line of work applies the dataset to different tasks. For instance, one paper uses it to evaluate models on image captioning, image generation, image reconstruction, and cross-modal retrieval. Thus, its primary role in the surveyed literature is as a benchmark for fine-grained visual recognition, with less frequent applications in generative and retrieval contexts.
