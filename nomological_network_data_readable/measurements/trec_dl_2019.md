**Type:** Measurement

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, TREC-DL 2019 is consistently described as a benchmark dataset or test set from the Text REtrieval Conference (TREC) Deep Learning Track of 2019. Its prevailing use is for evaluating information retrieval systems, with papers frequently applying it to assess performance on tasks such as passage retrieval and document reranking. The benchmark is used to evaluate various systems, including "LLM-based ranking systems" ("HMoE: Heterogeneous Mixture of Experts for Language Modeling") and systems for "deep learning-based ad-hoc retrieval tasks" ("From perception to production..."). Some sources specify its composition, noting it is "derived from MS-MARCO-V1" ("LLMs Behind the Scenes...") and contains "human-annotated relevance judgments" ("From perception to production..."). A few papers provide more specific context, with one characterizing its use as "in-domain evaluation" ("LLMs Behind the Scenes...") and another describing it as a dataset that is "not reasoning intensive" ("SAFENUDGE...").
