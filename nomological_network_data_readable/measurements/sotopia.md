# SOTOPIA
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

SOTOPIA is predominantly characterized across the provided literature as an open-ended benchmark environment designed to evaluate agents by simulating complex social interactions. The primary construct it is used to measure is social intelligence, a purpose stated in multiple definitions and a direct relationship link; one paper also reports its use for measuring strategic reasoning. The benchmark operationalizes these evaluations through interactive simulations where agents with specific character profiles and private goals engage in various scenarios. One paper specifies that these are structured as "social tasks, each defined by a scenario, two role profiles, and private goals." Another source provides more detail on its composition, noting it "includes 90 scenarios, each with 5 role pairs." A less common framing, found in one paper, describes SOTOPIA as a "platform that supports the simulation and evaluation of social interactions with LLM agents" and uses it as a foundation for a different framework.
