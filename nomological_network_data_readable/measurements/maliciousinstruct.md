# MaliciousInstruct
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 9 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, MaliciousInstruct is consistently characterized as a benchmark dataset composed of "malicious" or "harmful" instructions. Its primary purpose is to evaluate the safety of large language models, with papers using it to measure the construct of Safety. Specifically, it is employed to test a model's "safety defenses" and its "resistance... to harmful queries." A recurring theme is its application as an out-of-domain (OOD) or out-of-distribution test set, with one paper using it to "assess transferability" of defenses. One source specifies that the benchmark contains "100 broader harmful instructions." In evaluations, it is often used alongside other harmful benchmarks such as AdvBench and HEx-PHI.
