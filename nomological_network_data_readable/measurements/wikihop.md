# WikiHop
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  
**Also known as:** 2WikiHop, Wikihop  

## State of the Field

Across the provided sources, WikiHop (also cited as 2WikiHop) is consistently characterized as a benchmark dataset for multi-hop question answering. The definitions specify that it is built on Wikipedia and designed for tasks where answers require traversing and synthesizing information from multiple documents. The dataset is used to measure several capabilities, with its most direct application being the evaluation of multi-hop question answering performance. Additionally, some papers use WikiHop to operationalize the broader construct of reading comprehension, while another employs it to evaluate the specific effectiveness of web-page retrieval. In the context of evaluation, WikiHop is frequently mentioned and used alongside other multi-hop datasets, particularly HotpotQA and Musique. One study notes its use in experiments with a "FiD-Large as reader" model, illustrating a specific application context.
