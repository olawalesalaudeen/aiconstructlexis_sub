## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, T-REx is consistently characterized as a dataset used for evaluation. The prevailing description is that of a "slot filling dataset," a framing found in multiple papers. Its primary application is to evaluate a model's ability to perform "factual relation extraction-style prediction" from text or knowledge. One paper also uses it to measure the broader behavior of "Information extraction". While most definitions focus on its function, one provides more detail on its construction, describing it as a "large-scale slot-filling dataset derived from Wikipedia abstracts and aligned with Wikidata triples". Another source situates it within a larger collection of benchmarks, noting its inclusion in "the KILT subset and evaluation tasks".
