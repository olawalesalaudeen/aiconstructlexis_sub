## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, WMT14 is consistently used as an instrument for the task of `Machine translation`. It is most commonly framed as a 'machine translation benchmark', although it is also described as a 'dataset'. The prevailing use of the benchmark is to 'evaluate translation quality on standard translation directions'. A more specific application, noted in one paper, is for evaluating 'speculative decoding methods on translation text distributions'. Evidence from the source materials shows its application to specific language pairs, including German-English ('De-En', 'En-De') and an 'English-French subset'. Researchers utilize WMT14 for both training and evaluation, with some studies drawing training and test sets from it, while others use it explicitly as a corpus to train models for translation tasks.
