# COMET
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 12 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, COMET (Crosslingual Optimized Metric for Evaluation of Translation) is consistently characterized as a learned evaluation metric that employs neural models to assess translation quality. The most prevalent definition describes it as using multilingual representations, with several papers specifying that it evaluates aspects like "semantic adequacy" and "fluency." A less common framing describes it more broadly as a metric for scoring the quality of any generated text relative to a reference. In practice, COMET is deployed as a standard evaluation metric for translation tasks, frequently used alongside BLEU and BERTScore. One study notes the use of a specific version, "wmt22-comet-da," while another reports its results as a percentage. Its performance relative to other metrics is a point of discussion; one paper states that "COMET shows similar results as BLEU," while another finds that it "exhibits a stronger correlation with idiom translation compared to BLEU and BERTScore."
