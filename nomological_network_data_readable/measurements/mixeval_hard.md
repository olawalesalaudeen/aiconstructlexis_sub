# MixEval-hard
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  
**Also known as:** MixEval Hard, MixEval-Hard  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, MixEval-hard is consistently characterized as a benchmark for evaluating language models on difficult problems. The prevailing usage, found in multiple papers, defines it as a "challenging subset" of the MixEval benchmark, specifically designed to "test the limits of LLM capabilities on difficult queries." A slightly different framing describes it simply as a benchmark for "challenging general domain tasks." Its primary function is to assess model performance on these more demanding tasks. One paper uses MixEval-hard as a measurement instrument for the construct of Commonsense knowledge. To contextualize its evaluation power, one study notes that MixEval-Hard "shows a higher correlation of 0.96 with Chatbot Arena Elo" (Magnetic Preference Optimization: Achieving Last-iterate Convergence for Language Model Alignment).
