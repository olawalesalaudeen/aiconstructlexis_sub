# SIB-200
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided sources, SIB-200 is consistently presented as a measurement instrument for text classification. It is defined as a multilingual benchmark that spans 204 languages and multiple domains. Papers use SIB-200 to evaluate models on the sentence-level semantic task of topic classification, sometimes in a zero-shot setting. The stated evaluation goals are to assess both "classification accuracy" and "cross-domain generalization." One paper describes an operationalization of the latter, where models are trained on one domain and then evaluated on all other domains within the benchmark. The data also indicates that specific language subsets, such as Filipino and Cebuano, are sometimes used for evaluation.
