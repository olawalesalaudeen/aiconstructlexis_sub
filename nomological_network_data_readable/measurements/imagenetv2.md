# ImageNetV2
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  
**Also known as:** ImageNet-V2  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, ImageNetV2 is consistently characterized as a test set or benchmark derived from ImageNet. The papers offer slightly different but related framings of its purpose. One definition states it is used to test "robustness under natural distribution shift." Another paper describes it as a tool for measuring "generalization to new, similarly distributed data," specifying that it was collected with a replicated protocol. In terms of its application, it is used for model evaluations, sometimes in conjunction with other datasets. As one study notes, "We use the ImageNetV2, ImageNet-R, ImageNet-A, and ImageNetSketch datasets for these evaluations".
