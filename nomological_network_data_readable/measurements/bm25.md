# BM25
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 33 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, BM25 is consistently described as a sparse retrieval method, frequently characterized as a non-trainable, text-based algorithm based on probabilistic information retrieval models. Its most common function is to rank and select relevant items from a corpus based on a query, with applications ranging from retrieving tables and documents to selecting passages. A prevalent use case is in the context of in-context learning, where many studies employ BM25 to retrieve semantically similar exemplars or demonstrations from a training set. For instance, one paper uses it to "retrieve the top-k similar training samples as candidate samples" ("DQ-LoRe: Dual Queries with Low Rank Approximation Re-ranking for In-Context Learning"). BM25 is also frequently positioned as a baseline method for comparison against other retrieval techniques, such as TF-IDF and transformer-based approaches. Additionally, it is used in more specific applications, such as evaluating query expansion methods and testing the transfer of adversarial attacks to sparse retrievers. The provided data indicates that BM25 is used as an instrument in studies of Information Retrieval and Faithfulness.
