# ReXVal
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the available data, ReXVal is characterized as a dataset used for evaluating generated radiology reports. It is defined as containing both ground-truth and machine-generated reports that have been annotated by radiologists. These annotations identify six distinct categories of errors, which are further classified as clinically significant or insignificant. The primary application described for this dataset is to assess the clinical validity of other evaluation metrics. As one paper notes, this validation is operationalized by "computing Pearson Correlations between the metric’s scores and clinician annotations from the ReXVal dataset" ("D-CoDe: Scaling Image-PretrainedVLMs to Video via Dynamic Compression and Question Decomposition").
