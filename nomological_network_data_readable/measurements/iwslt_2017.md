## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, IWSLT 2017 is consistently described as a benchmark dataset used to evaluate machine translation capabilities. The definitions commonly specify its origin as the International Workshop on Spoken Language Translation 2017, with one paper noting its content consists of "parallel documents sourced from TED talks" ("DelTA: An Online Document-Level Translation Agent Based on Multi-Level Memory"). Its most prevalent application is to measure the behavior of `Machine translation`. While this is the dominant use case, a smaller set of studies employs the dataset for more specific investigations. For example, one paper uses it for "zero-shot prompting experiments" ("Uncertainty-Aware Decoding with Minimum Bayes Risk"). Another study utilizes it in the context of text translation to study correlations between model self-evaluation and established metrics such as BLEU, ROUGE, and BERTScore.
