# MAMI
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

The MAMI (Memes Are Misogynist, Huh?) dataset is consistently presented in the provided literature as a publicly available resource for evaluation. The dataset is characterized as containing multimodal abusive memes, with a specific focus on content that is "predominantly derogatory towards women" (acl25, emnlp25). One source further specifies that these memes exemplify "typical subjects of online vitriol" (acl25). Across the available data, MAMI's primary application is to evaluate "harmful content detection". A more specific framing describes its use for "benchmarking models' detection of abusive language and imagery in social media content" (emnlp25). The provided snippets show it being used in evaluation contexts alongside other meme datasets.
