# GaoKao
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  
**Also known as:** GAOKAO  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, GaoKao is consistently presented as a dataset derived from or simulating the Chinese National College Entrance Examination. Its primary application is to evaluate models on reasoning abilities within academic contexts. One paper specifies its use for assessing "complex reasoning in subjects like physics, chemistry, and math" ("Enhancing Automated Interpretability with Output-Centric Feature Descriptions"), while another describes it as an "exam-oriented dataset" requiring "high-level reasoning and knowledge integration" ("AdaptThink: Reasoning Models Can Learn When to Think"). Reinforcing this usage, other work employs GaoKao as a measurement instrument for mathematical reasoning. The dataset's focus is on Chinese academic content, as one source explicitly mentions its use for "Chinese physical, chemical, and mathematical reasoning" ("Enhancing Automated Interpretability with Output-Centric Feature Descriptions").
