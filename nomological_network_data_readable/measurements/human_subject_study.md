# Human subject study
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  
**Also known as:** Community survey, Offline human survey, User survey  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, human subject studies represent a versatile category of measurement methods that involve human participants in evaluating AI systems or collecting data. One common application is for quality assurance, where participants are asked to assess the "reliability and quality" of generated test cases and model responses. These studies are also operationalized as a "data collection procedure" to establish human performance baselines, for example by having participants perform a task to compute "humans’ raw accuracy". A prevalent use involves deploying surveys to gather perspectives from specific populations, such as surveying community members to identify "harmful model behaviours" or understanding "adoption patterns, use cases, and challenges" among users with visual impairments. The methodologies described vary, including "mixed-methods survey[s]" with "open-ended questions and Likert scale ratings" and procedures where participants submit guesses after viewing a sequence of clues.
