## State of the Field

Based on the provided data, ComplexWebQuestions, also referred to as CWQ, is a benchmark dataset characterized by its use of "multi-hop, web-based questions." The available sources consistently frame it as an instrument for testing reasoning abilities. One paper explicitly defines its purpose as testing a model's "reasoning ability over web content." In practice, it is used to measure several related constructs. The data shows it is used to operationalize and assess both "Complex reasoning" and "Commonsense understanding." Thus, across the surveyed work, the benchmark is positioned as a tool for evaluating different facets of reasoning over information from the web.
