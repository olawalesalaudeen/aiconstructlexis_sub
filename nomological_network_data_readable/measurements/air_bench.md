# AIR-Bench
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 14 papers  
**Also known as:** AirBench, AIR-bench, AIR-Benchmark, AIR-BENCH 2024  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, the name 'AIR-Bench' refers to several distinct benchmarks, leading to considerable variation in its definition and use. The most prevalent framing describes it as an aggregated evaluation suite for Large Audio Models, designed to measure audio understanding and general auditory processing across speech, music, and audio tasks. Some papers in this vein note that it extends to open-ended question-answering formats, while one specifies that its conversational evaluation component, AirBench-Chat, relies on text-based interactions. A separate line of work defines AIR-Bench as a benchmark for measuring information retrieval performance, particularly on out-of-domain topics, with one paper noting its test data is "automatically generated by large language models." A third, distinct characterization appears in a single paper, which presents 'AIR-BENCH 2024' as a language-model safety benchmark containing thousands of "risky instruction prompts directly grounded in the current regulatory landscape." Thus, depending on the paper, AIR-Bench is used to evaluate audio processing, information retrieval, or model safety.
