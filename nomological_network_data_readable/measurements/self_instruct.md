# Self-Instruct
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  
**Also known as:** Self-Instruct Eval, Self-instruct  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, Self-Instruct is consistently positioned as an instrument for evaluating language models, frequently appearing in lists of benchmarks alongside Vicuna, Koala, and Dolly Eval. Its primary purpose is described as testing model performance, specifically targeting generalization, response quality, and the capabilities of instruction-tuned models. There is notable disagreement in the definitions regarding its composition; most describe it as a dataset based on "self-instructed prompts" generated by a model itself, while one paper defines it as a test set of "human-generated instructions". A less common framing also presents Self-Instruct not only as an evaluation tool but as a "framework... for improving the instruction-following capabilities" of models. In terms of what it assesses, papers use Self-Instruct to measure the construct of Commonsense knowledge.
