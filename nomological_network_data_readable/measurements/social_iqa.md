# Social-IQa
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  
**Also known as:** Social IQa, Social IQA  

## State of the Field

Across the provided sources, Social-IQa is consistently characterized as a benchmark for evaluating social reasoning in models. It is operationalized as a multiple-choice question-answering dataset, with one paper noting that performance is measured by accuracy. The instrument is used to assess a model's ability to reason about people's actions, understand social situations, predict human reactions, and comprehend social implications. One paper specifically uses the dataset to study "social and emotional intelligence," stating that it contains questions about "people’s feelings" ("Divergences between Language Models and Human Brains"). While most sources frame it as an evaluation tool, the same paper also reports using Social-IQa for fine-tuning a model on social and emotional content. The definitions and usage contexts all position Social-IQa as an instrument for probing capabilities related to social cognition.
