# OLMES
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

OLMES is characterized in the provided literature as an evaluation suite for language models. It is defined as comprising multiple-choice and cloze-format tasks that cover knowledge and reasoning domains. One source specifies its composition, stating, "We adopt the OLMES suite... which includes the following tasks: MMLU, ARC-Easy, ARC-Challenge, CommonsenseQA, HellaSwag, OpenBookQA, PIQA, Social IQA, and WinoGrande." The definition also notes that the suite employs manually curated in-context examples and calibration methods to enhance reliability. In terms of its application, the available data shows that OLMES is used to measure the behavior of Text generation.
