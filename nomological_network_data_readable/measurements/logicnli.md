# LogicNLI
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, LogicNLI is characterized as a benchmark for evaluating logical reasoning. It is consistently used to measure constructs like "multi-step logical reasoning" and, more specifically, "first-order logic reasoning". The benchmark is structured as an NLI-style dataset, defined as a task of "hypothesis verification from facts and rules". A frequently mentioned design feature is its attempt to "isolate first-order logic reasoning from commonsense inference," which one paper suggests allows for a "precise logical evaluation." Containing 20k examples according to one source, it is often used in evaluations alongside other logical reasoning datasets such as RobustLR, ProofWriter, and LOGIQA.
