**Type:** Measurement

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, WMT19 is consistently described as a benchmark or dataset from the Workshop on Machine Translation 2019. Its prevailing use is to evaluate the performance of machine translation systems, as it is explicitly positioned as a measure for the behavior of "Machine translation". The benchmark is operationalized in a few different ways across the surveyed papers. Some studies utilize a range of its language pairs, such as "De–En, Fi–En, Lt–En, and Ru–En", for general translation evaluation. A more specific application is also documented, where one paper uses "the ‘Kazakh-English’ subset" to assess the "translation of minority languages" in the context of fine-tuning. The definitions are largely in agreement, framing it as a tool for evaluating machine translation, with variations in its application from broad performance testing to more targeted analyses on specific data subsets.
