# TableBench
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

TableBench is presented in the provided literature as a measurement instrument for evaluating model capabilities on tabular data. It is explicitly used to measure both "Table understanding" and "Table and chart reasoning". The benchmark is defined as "challenging," designed to assess performance across a "wide range of scenarios" by covering "diverse question types and topics," as noted in 'Seeing More, Saying More: Lightweight Language Experts are Dynamic Video Token Compressors'. In experimental contexts, it is sometimes used alongside other benchmarks such as WikiTableQuestions and HiTab.
