# VLMEvalKit
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 11 papers  
**Also known as:** VLMEvalkit  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, VLMEvalKit is consistently defined as an evaluation toolkit or standardized platform for assessing the performance of Vision-Language Models (VLMs), also referred to as Large Vision-Language Models (LVLMs). Its most common application is to run benchmark comparisons under consistent settings to facilitate fair evaluations across different methods. For instance, one study utilizes it to "ensure a fair comparison" (Dynamic Multimodal Evaluation with Flexible Complexity by Vision-Language Bootstrapping), while another employs it for "ensuring consistent configuration settings across all methods" (Parrot: Multilingual Visual Instruction Tuning). In addition to its use as an active evaluation tool, at least one paper treats VLMEvalKit as a data source for analysis. This work leverages the "comprehensive data from VLMEvalKit... which includes diverse benchmarks and performance results from more than 100 MLLMs" (AbGen: Evaluating Large Language Models in Ablation Study Design and Evaluation for Scientific Research). The provided data also indicates that VLMEvalKit is studied in relation to the MMBench-Video benchmark.
