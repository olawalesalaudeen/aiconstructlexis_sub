# JEEBench
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  
**Also known as:** JEEBENCH, JEE-Bench  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, JEEBench is consistently characterized as a benchmark dataset of mathematics problems used for evaluating large language models. Its primary stated purpose is to measure the construct of "Mathematical reasoning," with one paper describing its use for "evaluating mathematical reasoning on exam-style problems" (Omni-MATH: A Universal Olympiad Level Mathematic Benchmark for Large Language Models). The problems are consistently framed as "challenging." The content is described as covering "college-level mathematics topics," with one source specifying this includes "simple ODEs and multivariable calculus" (HARDMath: A Benchmark Dataset for Challenging Problems in Applied Mathematics). The benchmark is presented as one of several more difficult datasets proposed as language models have become more capable.
