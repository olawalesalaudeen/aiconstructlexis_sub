# C4
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 101 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, C4 (Colossal Clean Crawled Corpus) is consistently presented as a large-scale dataset derived from web crawls, with its most prevalent application being the evaluation of language model capabilities. The dominant use of C4 is to measure `language modeling` and `text generation`, which is almost universally operationalized through the perplexity metric, as seen in numerous papers on model quantization and pruning. A second, frequently mentioned role for C4 is as a source of calibration data for model compression techniques; for instance, one paper states, "We use C4 (Raffel et al., 2020) as the calibration dataset" ("SpQR: A Sparse-Quantized Representation for Near-Lossless LLM Weight Compression"). A smaller but notable body of work also utilizes C4 as a corpus for `language model pre-training` and continual training. Less commonly, papers employ C4 as a source of prompts for open-ended text generation tasks. The dataset is also occasionally used to evaluate other behaviors like `length generalization` and `robustness and stability`.
