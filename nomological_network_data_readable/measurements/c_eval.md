# C-Eval
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 17 papers  

## State of the Field

C-Eval is predominantly characterized as a comprehensive evaluation benchmark for assessing model capabilities in a Chinese context. The most common definition describes it as a tool for "multi-task language understanding in a cross-linguistic setting," and it is used in the literature to measure the construct of Generalization. Several sources specify its scope, noting it is a "comprehensive evaluation suite covering 52 Chinese academic subjects" across areas like STEM, humanities, and social sciences. Papers use C-Eval to evaluate a range of abilities, with a recurring focus on knowledge and reasoning. For instance, one study uses "the logic subject in the C-Eval dataset... to determine whether the model can understand and apply logical rules to make reasonable reasoning." Other stated applications include assessing general knowledge, problem-solving, commonsense reasoning, and Chinese instruction-following. While its primary focus is on Chinese, its utility in a "cross-linguistic scenario" is also noted. In practice, C-Eval is often included with benchmarks like MMLU and GSM8k to provide a "complete capability evaluation."
