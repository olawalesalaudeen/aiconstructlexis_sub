# MultiHopRAG
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

MultiHopRAG is characterized in the provided literature as a benchmark for evaluating multi-hop reasoning, specifically within retrieval-augmented generation settings. The benchmark's design requires the integration of information across multiple documents. One paper further specifies its purpose is to "assess the capability of LLMs to extract and synthesize relevant information from multiple documents" (MadaKV: Adaptive Modality-PerceptionKVCache Eviction for Efficient Multimodal Long-Context Inference). The available data indicates it is used to operationalize different reasoning constructs. For instance, one study employs MultiHopRAG to measure `Complex reasoning`, while another uses it to assess `Mathematical reasoning`.
