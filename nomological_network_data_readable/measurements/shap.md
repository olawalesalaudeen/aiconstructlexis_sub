**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Within the provided literature, SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) is characterized as an off-the-shelf feature attribution technique. Its use is specifically noted in the context of multilingual explainability. The source material suggests that such feature attribution techniques are "prevalent in low-resource language studies." One paper positions SHAP alongside LIME as an example of an "out-of-the-shelf explainability technique" that may be subject to "simplistic applications." This work also highlights broader "challenges in ensuring the faithfulness of explanations" associated with these methods.
