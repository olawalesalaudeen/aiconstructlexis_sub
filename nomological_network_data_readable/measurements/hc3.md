# HC3
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  
**Also known as:** Human ChatGPT Comparison Corpus (HC3)  

## State of the Field

The Human ChatGPT Comparison Corpus (HC3) is predominantly used as a benchmark for evaluating machine-generated text detection. Across the provided literature, it is consistently described as a dataset containing paired responses from both humans and ChatGPT (specifically GPT-3.5) to the same questions, often in a Q&A format. A frequently highlighted feature is its multiscale design, offering both full-text and sentence-level variants in English and Chinese to assess "multiscale detection performance." The short-text versions are specifically named "HC3-English-Sent and HC3-Chinese-Sent." While the prevailing application is for human-AI text discrimination tasks, such as training an encoder on its human-written texts, a less common use is also documented. One paper employs HC3 not for simple detection but to "validate... differences between ChatGPT and human responses" and compare their qualitative characteristics.
