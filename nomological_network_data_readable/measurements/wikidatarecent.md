# WikiDatarecent
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, WikiDatarecent is consistently presented as a measurement instrument for evaluating knowledge editing in large language models. The dataset is defined as focusing on "recent, updated facts from Wikidata." It is explicitly used to measure the behavior of `Knowledge editing`, with some papers specifying its application for tasks of "knowledge insertion and knowledge modification." In addition to its primary use, some work also employs WikiDatarecent to assess the constructs of `Locality` and `Linguistic quality` following an edit. In experimental contexts, it is often used alongside other datasets like ZsRE and WikiDatacounterfact.
