# ImageNet-R
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  
**Also known as:** Imagenet-R  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, ImageNet-R is consistently described as a benchmark for evaluating model performance. The most common framing positions it as a tool for assessing model robustness to out-of-distribution images. A related but more specific definition from one paper states it is designed to test generalization to novel styles. The benchmark's content is consistently characterized as consisting of various artistic renditions of ImageNet object classes. Examples provided in the source materials include 'paintings, cartoons, sculptures' and 'embroidery'.
