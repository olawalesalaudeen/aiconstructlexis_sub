# GSM-Hard
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 13 papers  
**Also known as:** GSM-HARD  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, GSM-Hard is consistently presented as a measurement instrument used to evaluate mathematical reasoning. It is uniformly described as a more challenging or difficult version of the GSM8K dataset, designed to test the limits of these capabilities. The papers offer slightly different characterizations of this increased difficulty; one definition states that the challenge comes from replacing numbers in the original GSM8k test set with "values of larger magnitude." Other sources describe it more generally as a subset containing "problems that state-of-the-art models struggle with" or as an "extended version of GSM8K with more complex arithmetic problems." One paper also notes its use for "out-of-distribution evaluation in math reasoning." It is shown to be used in evaluations alongside other mathematical tasks such as GSM8K, MATH, and SVAMP.
