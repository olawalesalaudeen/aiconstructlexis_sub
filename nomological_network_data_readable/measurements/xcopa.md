# XCOPA
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 14 papers  

## State of the Field

XCOPA is consistently described across the provided literature as a benchmark dataset for evaluating cross-lingual commonsense reasoning. The most prevalent framing specifies its focus on causal reasoning, with papers using it to measure the broader construct of "Commonsense understanding." The benchmark operationalizes this evaluation through a task requiring the selection of a plausible alternative, which is described as a multiple-choice or binary-choice classification problem. This task typically involves choosing the most plausible cause or effect given a premise. A recurring feature is its multilingual scope, with one paper noting it covers 11 languages. It is frequently studied alongside other reasoning datasets like XNLI and XStoryCloze and is sometimes evaluated using zero-shot prompting. While its multilingual coverage is a common theme, one paper characterizes its format as a "narrow" form of reasoning restricted to causal inferences.
