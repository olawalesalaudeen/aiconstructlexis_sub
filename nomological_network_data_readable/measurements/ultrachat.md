# UltraChat
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 15 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, UltraChat is most commonly characterized as a diverse, multi-turn conversational dataset used to operationalize and train for instruction following. The definitions consistently state it was generated by GPT-3.5-Turbo. Its prevailing application is in instruction fine-tuning, with several sources noting its use for this purpose, referring to it as a "large-scale dataset of instructional conversations" and a common "IFT dataset." A related framing, from one paper, positions it as a benchmark for "interactive dialogue." Beyond these primary uses, a smaller set of applications exists. For instance, one study employs UltraChat as a source of "benign" data to evaluate safety-patching procedures. Another paper reports its use for measuring commonsense knowledge, though this is a less frequent association in the provided data.
