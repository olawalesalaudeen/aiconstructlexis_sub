# TweetEval
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided sources, TweetEval is consistently defined as a multi-task benchmark designed for tweet analysis. It is operationalized as a collection of six specific text classification tasks: emotion, hate speech, irony, offensive language, sentiment, and stance detection. One paper notes these correspond to sub-benchmarks like "EmotionEval" and "HatEval" ("Steering off Course: Reliability Challenges in Steering Language Models"). While it encompasses multiple tasks, some research utilizes specific subsets, such as the emotion recognition and irony detection components ("Sparse Activation Editing for Reliable Instruction Following in Narratives"). Based on the available data, TweetEval is used as a measurement instrument to assess the broader construct of language understanding.
