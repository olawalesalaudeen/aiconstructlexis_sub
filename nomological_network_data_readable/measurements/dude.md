# DUDE
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, DUDE is consistently presented as a benchmark for multi-page document understanding. It is explicitly used to measure the construct of 'Long-context understanding', with one paper further specifying its use in evaluating retrieval and question-answering (QA) performance. The dataset is described as containing financial reports and is noted for its 'diverse domains and complex document structures' ("Enhancing Large Language Model for Knowledge Graph Completion via Structure-Aware Alignment-Tuning"). The task format is identified as multi-page visual question answering. A commonly mentioned characteristic is its focus on documents up to 20 pages; one study explicitly states that DUDE does 'not assess documents exceeding 20 pages' ("BIPro: Zero-shotChinese Poem Generation via Block Inverse Prompting Constrained Generation Framework"). In evaluations, it is sometimes used alongside other document understanding datasets such as MP-DocVQA.
