# SAT
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Within the provided literature, SAT is characterized as a standardized test, originally for U.S. college admissions, that has been repurposed to evaluate language model performance. Its primary documented use is as a benchmark to measure the construct of 'Mathematical reasoning'. Papers group SAT with other mathematics-related benchmarks like GSM8k and MATH to assess this capability across diverse domains and difficulty levels. The test is also used for comparing model performance against human levels, with one paper noting that a model's scores on the SAT are "comparable to those of human test-takers" ('A Mathematical Framework for AI-Human Integration in Work').
