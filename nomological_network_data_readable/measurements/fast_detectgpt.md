# Fast-DetectGPT
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 14 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, Fast-DetectGPT is consistently described as a procedure for determining whether a text is generated by a large language model. The method's mechanism is based on measuring probability curvature, referred to as either "conditional probability curvature" or "log-probability curvature" in different sources. One paper characterizes it as a "training-free" method, while another notes it is an "open-source" tool. Its application is framed in multiple ways; for instance, it is employed as a "safeguard for academic integrity" to classify machine-generated paper submissions. Other work uses it as a "representative training-free" baseline for comparison against other detection methods. In at least one evaluation, Fast-DetectGPT is reported to "struggle" in its detection performance when compared to other methods and human evaluators.
