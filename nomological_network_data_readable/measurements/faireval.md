# FairEval
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

FairEval is described in multiple distinct ways across the surveyed literature. The most prevalent framing, found in several papers, is as a benchmark dataset for evaluating open-ended question answering. In this capacity, it is reported to consist of 80 questions from diverse categories like "common-sense, counterfactual, coding" and is explicitly used to measure the "Open-ended question answering" behavior. A different paper presents FairEval as a "meta-evaluation benchmark" for assessing the performance and biases of LLM evaluators, noting its average human annotation accuracy is 71.7%. Yet another study frames it differently, as a "fairness-oriented evaluation procedure" adopted specifically to "eliminate positional bias." These varied descriptions show that FairEval is operationalized as a task-specific dataset, a tool for meta-evaluation, and a procedural method for assessment.
