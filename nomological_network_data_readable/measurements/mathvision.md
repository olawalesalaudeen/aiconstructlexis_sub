# MathVision
**Type:** Measurement  
**Referenced in:** 9 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, MathVision is consistently characterized as a dataset and benchmark used to evaluate visual mathematical reasoning in multimodal settings. The prevailing use of this instrument is to measure a model's reasoning ability on what are described as "math diagrams and visual math problems." The dataset is defined as covering topics such as algebra, geometry, combinatorics, and logic, requiring models to solve quantitative problems based on visual inputs. One paper notes that MathVision focuses on "standard mathematical tasks," which may distinguish it from other benchmarks. Performance is evaluated using accuracy on these complex multimodal problems, and one study mentions using a specific "testmini subset" for its analysis. In practice, MathVision is frequently employed alongside a suite of other visual reasoning benchmarks, most commonly MathVista, and also appears with MathVerse, Dynamath, and CharXiv in evaluations.
