# Hallucination resistance
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the available data from a single study, hallucination resistance is framed as a latent trait of a model concerning its ability to ground its outputs in source material. The provided definition specifies this as the avoidance of generating or endorsing "factually unsupported or fabricated knowledge triples not grounded in the source text." This construct is discussed within the particular application of constructing Knowledge Graphs (KGs) using LLMs, where the source material notes the hallucination phenomenon "cannot be overlooked." The operational evidence for a lack of this resistance is the generation of incorrect triples, such as one paper's example of "<AI Technology, replaces, Chemotherapy>" being generated without any reference in the original document. The study also mentions a method for improving this trait by using a module to filter inaccurate triples, thereby enhancing the quality of the resulting KGs.
