# Data diversity
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, data diversity is commonly defined as the extent of nonredundant and informative variation within a dataset. One framing specifies this variation relative to what a model has already learned, while another describes it more broadly as a latent characteristic reflecting the "breadth and variability of its content." The construct is operationalized through mechanisms described as "diversity-checkers." As one study explains, a low value from such a checker indicates a data point is less redundant and contains more information, thus contributing to higher diversity. This concept is applied in the context of evaluating both LLM-generated text and multimodal data. Its role is also contrasted with data quality, with one paper proposing that data diversity has a more pronounced effect for image-to-text models compared to text-to-video or image-text pre-training models.
