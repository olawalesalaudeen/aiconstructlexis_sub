# Professionalism
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided data, Professionalism is defined as a latent attribute of pre-training text, indicating the extent to which it contains professional knowledge. One paper specifies this knowledge as the type found in "textbooks or research articles" and frames it as a dimension that "serves as an indicator of the level of professional knowledge contained in the text" ("PrivacyRestore: Privacy-Preserving Inference in Large Language Models via Privacy Removal and Restoration"). This work also suggests that Professionalism supports advanced model capabilities in knowledge-intensive tasks. A separate paper proposes a causal relationship where human preference alignment is reported to cause Professionalism. The provided materials do not contain information on how this construct is operationalized or measured.
