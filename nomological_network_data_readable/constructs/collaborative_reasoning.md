# Collaborative reasoning
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

As characterized in the provided literature, collaborative reasoning is framed as a latent and inherent ability of large language models. The single available definition describes it as the capacity for multiple LLM agents to participate in a structured debate to collectively analyze a problem and arrive at a more accurate judgment. This construct is operationalized through the use of multi-agent debates, which are described as a method to "harness the collaborative reasoning capabilities of LLMs" (A Systematic Study of Compositional Syntactic Transformer Language Models). Within this work, the concept is situated alongside other inherent LLM abilities, such as knowledge and general reasoning.
