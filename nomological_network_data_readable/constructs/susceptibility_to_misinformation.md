# Susceptibility to misinformation
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 2 papers  
**Also known as:** Authority bias  

## State of the Field

The degree to which a large language model is likely to accept and incorporate false information provided by a user, even when it contradicts the model's internal knowledge.

## Sources

**[Scaling Laws and Efficient Inference for Ternary Language Models](https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.1295.pdf)**
> The degree to which a large language model is likely to accept and incorporate false information provided by a user, even when it contradicts the model's internal knowledge.

**[Know Your Mistakes: Towards Preventing Overreliance on Task-Oriented ConversationalAIThrough Accountability Modeling](https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.1400.pdf) (as "Authority bias")**
> The tendency of a large language model to favor knowledge provided by a human user over conflicting information from a database, even when the user's information is factually incorrect.
