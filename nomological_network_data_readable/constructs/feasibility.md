**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

In the context of scientific hypothesis generation, Feasibility is defined as "the extent to which a generated scientific hypothesis can be practically tested or validated through empirical methods" (SafeKey: Amplifying Aha-Moment Insights for Safety Reasoning). The available data operationalizes this construct through human assessment, with one paper noting its use in generating hypotheses with "high feasibility and impact as judged by human experts." Across the provided literature, Feasibility is measured by Human evaluation. This framing positions it as a quality of a scientific hypothesis that is determined by its potential for empirical validation.
