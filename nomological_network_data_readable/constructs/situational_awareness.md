# Situational awareness
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 9 papers  
**Also known as:** Situation awareness  

## State of the Field

The most prevalent framing of situational awareness in the provided literature is a model's knowledge of itself and its circumstances. This is commonly broken down into three components: the model's self-knowledge, its ability to make inferences about its situation, and its capacity to act on this knowledge. In this context, it is studied alongside constructs like `Self-reflection` and `Commonsense knowledge`. A distinct line of work defines situational awareness in the context of embodied AI, describing it as an agent's latent ability to understand its multi-modal local context within a 3D scene. This latter view is operationalized using the MSQA evaluation benchmark, which is designed "to precisely assess models’ situation awareness." Other papers offer more specialized definitions, such as the capacity to manage uncertainty during reasoning trajectories or the ability to perceive environmental and social cues in dialogue scenes. Some papers report that situational awareness influences other capabilities, such as `Situated reasoning` and response `Consistency`.
