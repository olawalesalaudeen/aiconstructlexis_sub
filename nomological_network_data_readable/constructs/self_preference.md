# Self-preference
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, "Self-preference" is consistently characterized as a latent bias in large language models. The prevailing framing describes this as a tendency for an LLM, when "serving as a judge," to systematically favor its own generated responses over those from other models. This preference is noted to occur independent of the actual quality of the outputs, with one paper describing it as a "latent cognitive bias" and a "phenomenon inherent in the model itself." To operationalize this construct, one paper proposes the "DBG score." This score is designed to measure self-preference by calculating "the difference between the scores assigned by the judge model to its own responses and the corresponding gold judgments." The focus of the provided work is on measuring this bias within the context of LLM-based judgments.
