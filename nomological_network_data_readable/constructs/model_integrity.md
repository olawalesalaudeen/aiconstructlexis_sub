# Model integrity
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

As presented in the surveyed literature, 'Model integrity' is a construct concerning the preservation of a model's capabilities after it has undergone unlearning. It is defined as "the degree to which a model maintains its overall performance and efficacy after undergoing unlearning procedures" (Unified Parameter-Efficient Unlearning for LLMs). The concept is framed around the objective of "maintaining the overall integrity and efficacy of the models" during this process. The desired outcome is to perform a wide range of unlearning tasks "without compromising model performance" (Unified Parameter-Efficient Unlearning for LLMs).
