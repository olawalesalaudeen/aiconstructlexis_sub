# Proactivity
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  
**Also known as:** Proactiveness, Initiating collaboration  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, Proactivity is predominantly characterized as a conversational agent's tendency to take initiative rather than being purely reactive. The most common definition describes it as an agent's ability to guide a user towards a goal, with some papers noting this involves planning ahead and guiding the conversation flow. A more specific framing defines it as the tendency to initiate topic shifts or provide unsolicited information, sometimes in the context of a "proactive transition between task and chitchat". A distinct line of work, using the term "Initiating collaboration," conceptualizes it as an agent's ability to recognize its own limitations and request assistance from other agents. The construct is operationalized in several ways; one paper reports measuring it through the accuracy of "proactive actions (Acc P)", while another source indicates that a Reward model is used for measurement. Proactivity is also studied in relation to Collaboration, with one paper noting that models are often better at "responding to collaboration than initiating collaboration."
