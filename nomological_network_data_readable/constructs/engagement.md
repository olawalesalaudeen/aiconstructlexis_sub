# Engagement
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, Engagement is most commonly defined as a model's ability to generate responses that are interesting, captivating, and likely to elicit further user interaction. A similar framing, appearing in one paper, describes it as the perceived quality of responses in holding a user's attention. A distinct, less common definition treats engagement as a measure of user behavior, combining metrics like reciprocity and discussion length, with one paper noting that it "combines interest and participation" (Improving Large Language Models Function Calling and Interpretability via Guided-Structured Templates). The construct is operationalized in several ways. Some work uses direct human evaluation, with one paper specifying the use of "observer agents... tasked with rating responses for alignment and engagement" (Training Socially Aligned Language Models on Simulated Social Interactions). Other approaches use automated evaluation via models like GPT-4o or proxy metrics such as a "dialog engagement classifier" and a "Reddit upvote probability ranking model" (Leftover Lunch: Advantage-based Offline Reinforcement Learning for Language Models). Engagement is frequently studied as one of several desirable model attributes, listed alongside concepts like honesty, helpfulness, and harmlessness as a "fine-grained alignment perspective," as well as with qualities like human-likeness and memorability.
