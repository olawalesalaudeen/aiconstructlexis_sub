# Emotional alignment
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Emotional alignment is a construct with differing definitions across the provided literature. The most prevalent framing, presented in "Apathetic or Empathetic? Evaluating LLMs' Emotional Alignments with Humans," defines it as "the degree to which an LLM's emotional responses to specific situations correspond with those of humans." The authors of this paper position their work as the first to establish and evaluate this concept. An alternative definition appears in "Data Drives Unstable Hierarchical Generalization inLMs," which characterizes it as the degree to which an AI's responses are "affectively appropriate and supportive in emotionally sensitive conversations." This latter view is associated with behaviors such as "providing advisory responses" and implementing "tone moderation and content adjustment." According to the provided data, the construct is operationalized and measured using Human evaluation. The concept is presented as an under-explored area, with one paper noting that "few address emotional alignment."
