# Exploration efficiency
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, exploration efficiency is a construct characterizing the quality of an agent's search process during learning or tuning. One definition frames it as the degree to which a model generates "sufficiently diverse trajectories to support continued learning and improvement." This view is associated with the "exploration-exploitation tradeoff" and the prevention of what one paper calls "diversity collapse." A second, more specific definition describes it as the extent to which an agent's exploration focuses on "meaningful, action-altering variations rather than redundant or ineffective changes." This latter framing emphasizes a "targeted and efficient" process that prioritizes changes with a "higher likelihood of meaningful impact on action." Both perspectives highlight the need to find a "balance between per-iteration improvement and exploration efficiency" to optimize overall performance.
