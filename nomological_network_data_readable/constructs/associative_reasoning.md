# Associative reasoning
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  
**Also known as:** Associative ability, Association thinking  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, associative reasoning is framed as a latent ability in models, though definitions vary. The most prevalent view defines it as the capacity to form "novel connections between seemingly unrelated concepts," a process considered a "foundation of creativity." A related definition focuses on generating "semantically meaningful and contextually appropriate non-literal connections." A distinct framing treats it as the ability to "connect disparate pieces of information through shared entities or concepts to answer complex, multi-hop queries." This construct is most commonly operationalized and measured using the AssoCiAm benchmark, which was designed to "quantify models’ associative abilities." In addition to its connection with creativity, which it is reported to drive, one study reports a "strong positive correlation between cognition and association." The construct is also studied alongside complex reasoning.
