# Knowledge utilization
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  
**Also known as:** Knowledge application ability, Expert knowledge application  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, "Knowledge utilization" is most commonly defined as the degree to which a model accesses and applies its inherent, pre-trained knowledge during task performance. Alternative framings also appear, with some work defining it as the capacity to apply newly learned facts, and others focusing specifically on specialized, domain-specific knowledge. A distinct usage, sometimes co-occurring with the pre-trained knowledge definition, treats it as the ability to leverage information provided within the input prompt or retrieved context. The construct is operationalized through specific benchmarks; for example, the DDXPlus dataset is used to assess the application of expert knowledge. Research aims to "strengthen their utilization of knowledge autonomously" (AlphaMath Almost Zero), though one study notes this ability can be "unstable and even counterintuitive" (Delving into the Reversal Curse). Another paper suggests that in-context learning "enhances the... knowledge utilization capabilities" of models (AISees YourLocation).
