# Evaluation capability
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  
**Also known as:** Evaluation  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, evaluation capability is consistently framed as a model's latent ability to assess the quality, correctness, or authenticity of generated content. The scope of this assessment varies across papers, with some defining it in the context of reasoning and planning, where the model evaluates its own "generated trajectory or its intermediate steps" to identify mistakes. Other work applies the concept to multimodal large language models (MLLMs), describing it as the ability to "make judgments based on criteria and standards, like assessing image quality or discerning content authenticity." A more specific framing treats this as the capacity of base LLMs to function as evaluators, with their accuracy being a primary concern. Although no specific measurement instruments are mentioned, the papers conceptualize its operationalization in a few ways. One approach suggests the capability is reflected in a model's probability distribution and its effectiveness in distinguishing successful from failed outputs. Another common operationalization involves directly measuring a model's assessment accuracy against human judgments, particularly for instruction-following tasks.
