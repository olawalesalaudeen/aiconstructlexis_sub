# Multilingual understanding
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  
**Also known as:** Cross-lingual understanding, Multilingual comprehension, Cross-language understanding, Multilingual perception and comprehension  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, multilingual understanding is predominantly defined as a model's latent capacity to process, comprehend, and perform tasks across multiple natural languages. A frequent emphasis is placed on including underrepresented, understudied, and low-resource languages, with some papers noting that model performance is often worse in these contexts compared to high-resource languages like English. Several papers extend this concept to multimodal settings, evaluating what they term "multilingual perception and comprehension" in tasks involving video, images, and tabular data. The preservation of cultural and linguistic nuance is also a recurring theme, with measurement instruments like ViMUL-Bench and a benchmark for Indian culture being used to operationalize this aspect. While most definitions focus on natural language, a distinct minority framing treats "cross-language understanding" as the ability to map semantic and structural equivalences between different programming languages for code translation. The construct is operationalized through various benchmarks, including those for cross-lingual understanding generally, as well as more specific instruments like TableEval for multilingual tabular data and ViMUL-Bench for video comprehension.
