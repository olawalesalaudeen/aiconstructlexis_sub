# Semantic drift
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

In the provided literature, semantic drift is characterized in two related contexts. The most specific definition describes it as the "gradual divergence in meaning that occurs across successive translations" due to "cumulative errors" in fidelity ("A Case Against Implicit Standards..."). In this framing, it is positioned as a construct that influences `Translation quality`, with one study noting how "meaning gradually shifts from a factual query to subjective concern" over iterative translations. A different line of work discusses semantic drift alongside `Model collapse`, defining it more broadly as the idea that "the influx of AI-generated text can shift the distribution and meaning of language" ("Refining Text Generation for Realistic Conversational Recommendation via Direct Preference Optimization"). Both conceptualizations identify "compounding errors" as a central mechanism driving the shift in meaning. The provided data does not specify a direct measurement instrument for this construct.
