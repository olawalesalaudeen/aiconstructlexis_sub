# Warmth
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, the construct of Warmth is framed in two distinct ways. One line of work defines it specifically in the context of human-chatbot interaction as "the extent to which the chatbot conveys empathy and emotional connection." A different framing presents it more broadly as a dimension of social perception, concerning "the perceived intent or social friendliness dimension of an impression." In this latter context, Warmth is studied alongside Competence, with one paper noting that these two dimensions "account for 54-63% of the variance in LLM-generated stereotypes." The construct is operationalized through user evaluations, as one study reports that "chatbots integrating an AA accent with AAE dialect features tend to receive positive ratings across various characteristics... particularly Warmth." This suggests Warmth is used both to assess the affective quality of conversational agents and to analyze the social biases present in language models.
