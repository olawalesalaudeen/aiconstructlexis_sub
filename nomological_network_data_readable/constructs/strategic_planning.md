# Strategic planning
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 8 papers  
**Also known as:** Strategy planning, Multi-turn planning, Strategic thinking  

## State of the Field

The most prevalent definition of strategic planning across the provided literature describes it as a latent ability to navigate complex decision spaces by simulating future outcomes, balancing exploration and exploitation, and refining actions to achieve long-term goals. A smaller set of papers offers a more specific framing, using terms like "strategy planning" and "strategic thinking" to characterize the capability to devise and execute a series of actions within multi-turn dialogues, particularly to guide participants with conflicting interests toward a consensus or to maximize information extraction. Operationalization of this construct varies with context. In general decision-making tasks, it is associated with foresight in complex environments involving resource management and adversarial actions, as seen in the game-based setting of CivRealm. One paper, "PromptAgent," operationalizes it via a Monte Carlo tree search algorithm to "strategically navigate the vast prompt space." In dialogue-oriented tasks, it is measured by an LLM's capacity to structure questions over time, persuade sources, and select appropriate strategies. The provided data indicates only one associated construct, noting that strategic planning is related to commonsense knowledge.
