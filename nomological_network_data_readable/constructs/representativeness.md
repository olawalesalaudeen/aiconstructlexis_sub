# Representativeness
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided literature, Representativeness is a construct defined as the degree to which a subset of training data captures the essential information from the full dataset, reflecting both sufficiency and necessity. This concept is framed in the context of selecting a "core subset of demonstrations" from a larger training set. The stated goals for creating such a representative subset are to maintain data quality while also minimizing computational expenses, resulting in what one paper calls a "faithful representation" that is as minimal in size as possible. In terms of measurement, the construct is operationalized in one study through the use of the OpinionQA instrument.
