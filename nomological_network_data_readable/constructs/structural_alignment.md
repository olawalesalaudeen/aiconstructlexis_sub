# Structural alignment
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Structural alignment is presented in the surveyed work as a latent ability of a model to recognize and leverage compositional, tree-like syntactic or logical structure similarity between inputs and exemplars. This capacity is defined in opposition to surface-level semantics, with the source material distinguishing between "semantic-only and structure-aware similarity". The concept is situated within the context of in-context learning (ICL), where it is used to inform exemplar selection strategies. One study argues that overlooking structural alignment in this context leads to "suboptimal performance and poor generalization" and proposes a method that selects exemplars based on being "both semantically relevant and structurally aligned".
