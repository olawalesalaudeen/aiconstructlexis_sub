# Cognitive ability
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  
**Also known as:** Cognitive capability, Cognitive capacity, Socio-emotional skills, Executive skills  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, 'Cognitive ability' is a construct with several distinct framings. The most detailed definition presents it as a general mental capacity encompassing knowledge, perception, and reasoning, which is theorized to be foundational to associative ability. Another perspective defines it as 'cognitive capacity,' an inherent, unobservable limit on a model's ability for complex reasoning that is often correlated with its parameter size. A third line of work disaggregates the concept into more specific sub-types, including 'Executive skills' for managing task demands and 'Socio-emotional skills' for understanding social interactions. The construct is operationalized and measured using various benchmarks. For instance, the MMMU benchmark is explicitly used to evaluate 'comprehensive cognitive skills.' More specific facets are assessed with other instruments; LogiQA 2.0 is used to measure executive skills, while EQ-Bench is used for socio-emotional skills. Cognitive ability is reported to have a 'strong positive correlation' with associative reasoning and is also studied in relation to mathematical reasoning.
