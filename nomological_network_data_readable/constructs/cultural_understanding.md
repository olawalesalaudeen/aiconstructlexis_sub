# Cultural understanding
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 34 papers  
**Also known as:** Cultural awareness  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, cultural understanding is most commonly defined as an ability to comprehend and reference shared cultural knowledge, norms, or contexts. A less frequent but related framing, termed "cultural awareness," focuses more on navigating social interactions consistent with a culture's social norms. The construct is operationalized and measured using benchmarks such as CVQA and LITRANSPROQA, the latter of which is explicitly noted to assess cultural understanding in literary contexts. A dominant theme is the evaluation of this capability in specific, often non-English or non-Western, sociocultural environments, with papers assessing model performance on Japanese, Italian, Indian, Arabic, Persian, Thai, and Kazakh cultural knowledge. These evaluations cover diverse domains, including humor, proverbs, regional cuisines, and local history. Several papers find that models exhibit an "inadequate" or "shallow understanding" in these contexts, with one study noting a tendency to "rely on surface-level details rather than synthesizing contextual clues" when identifying Persian cultural concepts. The construct is also documented as being related to societal bias.
