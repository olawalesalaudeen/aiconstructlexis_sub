# Big Five personality traits
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  
**Also known as:** Agreeableness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Neuroticism, Extroversion  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, the Big Five personality traits are consistently framed as a set of five broad, adaptive dimensions for describing personality: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion (also termed Extroversion), Agreeableness, and Neuroticism. The primary operationalization of these constructs involves analyzing the behaviors of LLMs to provide a "quantitative interpretation of the values and personality traits of LLMs" ("Personality Alignment of Large Language Models"). To achieve this, researchers use measurement instruments such as the IPIP-NEO-120 and IPIP-NEO-300 questionnaires to quantify these behavioral patterns. The trait of Conscientiousness receives particular attention, being associated with "diligence and carefulness" and is reported to positively impact a model’s ability to handle complex reasoning tasks. Another study notes that "activating neurons associated with the positive aspect of conscientiousness leads to improvement in all tasks" ("Neuron based Personality Trait Induction in Large Language Models"). The provided sources consistently ground their approaches in this five-factor model.
