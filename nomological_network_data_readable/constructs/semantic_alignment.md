# Semantic alignment
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 21 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, "Semantic alignment" is predominantly framed as a construct in text-to-visual generation, though a separate line of work defines it in the context of textual embeddings. The most common usage describes it as the degree to which generated visual content accurately corresponds to the semantic concepts and relationships in a text prompt. In this context, it is studied alongside `Image generation` and is associated with achieving "semantically coherent" and compositionally correct visual outputs, with one paper noting that "better text-video alignment can benefit overall consistency" ("VideoTetris: Towards Compositional Text-to-Video Generation"). A different conceptualization treats semantic alignment as a model's latent ability to encode related meanings between paired texts into "aligned vector representations" ("DIDS: Domain Impact-aware Data Sampling for Large Language Model Training"). This framing is concerned with producing "richer semantic representations" and "higher-quality embeddings." The construct's association with `Theorem proving` suggests its relevance is also explored in tasks requiring alignment between different symbolic or textual formats. This dual usage highlights its application both in evaluating the faithfulness of generative visual models and in measuring the quality of learned text representations.
