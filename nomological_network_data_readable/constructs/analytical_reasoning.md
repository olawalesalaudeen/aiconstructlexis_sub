# Analytical reasoning
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided data, analytical reasoning is characterized as a capacity for in-depth analysis of complex documents. The single available definition describes it as the ability to draw meaningful conclusions from content, particularly over financial, technical, or academic data ("VLASCD: A Visual Language Action Model for Simultaneous Chatting and Decision Making"). This capacity is framed as a challenging aspect required for the "full comprehension of long multimodal documents" and is mentioned alongside domain knowledge and visual interpretation. The provided materials do not specify how this construct is operationalized or measured. In other work, analytical reasoning is studied in relation to both mathematical reasoning and commonsense understanding ("Self-DC: When to Reason and When to Act? Self Divide-and-Conquer for Compositional Unknown Questions").
