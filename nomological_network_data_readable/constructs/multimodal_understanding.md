## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, multimodal understanding is predominantly framed as a latent ability or capacity to integrate and interpret information from multiple modalities, with the most common pairing being vision and language. While this vision-text focus is prevalent, a smaller body of work extends the concept to include audio, video, speech, and specialized data types such as 3D molecular structures. This construct is most commonly operationalized and measured through model performance on a wide array of benchmarks, with MMBench, MME, MMMU, and VQA-v2 appearing frequently as evaluation instruments. The tasks used to assess this ability are consistently centered on visual question answering and image captioning. Some papers conceptualize the underlying mechanism as "multimodal alignment" or "multimodal fusion," focusing on how a model creates a shared semantic space from different inputs. A few studies also posit that this construct influences downstream capabilities like multimodal generation.
