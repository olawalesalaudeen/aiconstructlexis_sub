# Multi-image understanding
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Within the provided literature, multi-image understanding is consistently defined as a latent ability of models to correctly interpret and reason over inputs containing multiple images and interleaved text. This capacity is framed as a target for enhancement in Large Vision-Language Models (LVLMs), with one paper describing it as a commonly discussed direction for future development. To operationalize and assess this ability, researchers employ a range of evaluation benchmarks. The most frequently cited instruments for measuring multi-image understanding in this set of papers are Mantis-Eval, NLVR2, and BLINK. Other benchmarks are also used to evaluate this construct, including MMMU, MVBench, MathVerse-mv, and SciVerse-mv. Additionally, one paper introduces MUIRBENCH as a benchmark specifically “designed to rigorously assess and evaluate multi-image understanding by multimodal LLMs.” The available data shows a consistent approach to conceptualizing and measuring this construct, focusing on model performance in multi-image scenarios.
