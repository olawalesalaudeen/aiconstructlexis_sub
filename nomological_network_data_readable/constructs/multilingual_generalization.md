# Multilingual generalization
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 11 papers  
**Also known as:** Generalization to unseen languages  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, Multilingual generalization is predominantly defined as a model's ability to apply its capabilities to languages it was not explicitly trained on, particularly those with low resource availability. This concept is also referred to as 'cross-lingual generalization' and 'generalization to unseen languages', with one paper explicitly connecting it to 'strong zero-shot performance' in diverse linguistic contexts. A less common definition extends the idea of 'zero-shot generalization' to unseen time series categories, though the primary focus in this set of papers is linguistic. The construct is operationalized by measuring model performance across a wide array of benchmarks, including WikiText, LAMBADA, AG News, ScienceQA, MMBench, GSM, and GrailQA. Some research connects this ability to broader concerns, such as achieving 'cultural grounding' and creating 'linguistically inclusive AI systems' to address 'systemic gaps in training data'. Multilingual generalization is also studied alongside cross-domain generalization and is reported to be influenced by both Commonsense knowledge and Compositionality.
