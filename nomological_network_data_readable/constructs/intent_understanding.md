# Intent understanding
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 15 papers  
**Also known as:** Pragmatic reasoning  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, this construct is most commonly described as the ability to infer meaning beyond literal linguistic content, a capacity frequently labeled as either "Intent understanding" or "Pragmatic reasoning". The prevailing view defines it as inferring a speaker's underlying purpose or goal by integrating context, world knowledge, and social or cultural norms. This is exemplified by capabilities such as "interpreting indirect requests and non-literal meanings" and "implicature resolution," as noted in one study. One paper mentions that this ability is studied through the "Wavelength" setting. A less common framing extends the concept from conversational contexts to inferring a user's goal from their interactions with a graphical user interface, where it is also referred to as "goal understanding". In the surveyed work, this construct is positioned as related to `Theory of mind` and `Text-based reasoning`. Additionally, it is reported to be a cause of `Interpretability and explainability`.
