# Data quality
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided data, data quality is defined as the extent to which training examples are accurate, informative, and aligned with human preferences. This framing consistently contrasts quality with quantity, with one paper noting that "the quality of instruction data is more important than its quantity." The definition further specifies that data quality influences model performance more than the sheer volume of data. The provided materials discuss the use of "high-quality instruction data" but do not offer specific methods for its measurement or operationalization.
