**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided data, statistical reasoning is characterized within a single study as a proficiency in quantitative synthesis using information from multiple documents. The definition specifies that this proficiency includes aggregation, distributional analysis, correlation analysis, and variance analysis. This construct is operationalized through statistical tasks designed to assess these capabilities in Large Language Models. In the context of its source paper, "LLMBias Detection and Mitigation through the Lens of Desired Distributions", statistical reasoning is presented as one of three primary reasoning categories within a benchmark, alongside comparative and relational reasoning.
