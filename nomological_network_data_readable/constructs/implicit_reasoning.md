# Implicit reasoning
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided data, `Implicit reasoning` is defined as a model's capacity to infer unstated assumptions, background knowledge, or indirect logical connections to interpret ambiguous or incompletely specified argumentative content. This framing, originating from a single paper, presents the construct as a component for "identifying a logical structure" by "utilizing different background knowledge to complement implicit information" (MOSAIC: Modeling SocialAIfor Content Dissemination and Regulation in Multi-Agent Simulations). The same work notes that a model can sometimes identify "implicit logic that even annotators did not realize." The provided materials do not specify how this construct is operationalized or measured. In the context of other work, `Implicit reasoning` is studied alongside `Complex reasoning`. A single paper also posits a directional relationship, suggesting that the phenomenon of `Grokking` causes `Implicit reasoning`.
