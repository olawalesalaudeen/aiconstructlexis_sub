# Speech quality
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

In the provided literature, speech quality is defined as the perceptual quality of synthesized speech output, encompassing aspects such as naturalness, clarity, and the absence of artifacts. This construct is consistently operationalized and measured through specific automated evaluation tools. Across the surveyed papers, UTMOS is a common instrument used to evaluate it, described as assessing "overall speech quality" and, more specifically, the "naturalness" of the speech by predicting a Mean Opinion Score (MOS). Other papers also report using ViSQOL and MOSNet to compute the quality of reconstructed speech. Thus, the concept is treated as a property of generated audio that can be quantified by these specialized models.
