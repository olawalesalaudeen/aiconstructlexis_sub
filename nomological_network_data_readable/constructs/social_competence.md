# Social competence
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  
**Also known as:** Social awareness, Social understanding, Humanistic care  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, social competence is a construct defined through several related lenses, including social awareness, social understanding, and humanistic care. The prevailing usage frames it as a latent quality for handling social situations appropriately, such as a conversational agent avoiding "socially deficient replies" ("Proceedings of the 2025 Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing"). Other definitions extend this to include the ability to handle "socially grounded judgments or commonsense social situations" ("Understanding and Leveraging the Expert Specialization of Context Faithfulness in Mixture-of-ExpertsLLMs") and to interpret "subtle, implicit social cues in human communication" beyond literal text ("Instructing Large Language Models for Low-Resource Languages: A Systematic Study forBasque"). A more specialized framing appears as "humanistic care," which focuses on respectful and empathetic communication in specific contexts. To operationalize this construct, the literature reports using the SocialIQA benchmark as a measurement instrument, suggesting that performance on commonsense social tasks is used as evidence for this capability.
