# Text understanding
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  
**Also known as:** Text-level understanding  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, Text understanding is framed in at least two distinct ways, with no single dominant definition. One perspective treats it as a general ability to "comprehend the meaning, nuance, and details within a given text, particularly long or complex descriptions" ("What If We Recaption Billions of Web Images with LLaMA-3?"). In contrast, a more domain-specific framing defines it as the "latent ability of the LLM to comprehend and reason about neural architecture search tasks through natural language descriptions" ("RZ-NAS..."). In this latter context, the construct is operationalized by providing models with textual descriptions of a search space to guide architecture mutation. More broadly, the construct is measured using the LAMBADA benchmark in at least one study. The concept is also studied alongside and considered related to Natural language understanding.
