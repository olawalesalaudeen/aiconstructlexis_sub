# Aesthetic quality
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  
**Also known as:** Visual quality  

## State of the Field

In the provided literature, "Aesthetic quality" is conceptualized in two distinct ways. One definition frames it broadly, encompassing the "visual appeal, artistic merit, and overall quality of a generated image" (AP-Adapter: Improving Generalization of Automatic Prompts on Unseen Text-to-Image Diffusion Models). A second, more technical framing, also referred to as "visual quality," defines it as a latent property concerning "clarity, resolution, brightness, and absence of artifacts" in generated content (GenAI Arena: An Open Evaluation Platform for Generative Models). Across these sources, it is consistently treated as a desirable property of generated media that models should achieve. For instance, one paper repeatedly emphasizes the need to ensure both "semantic consistency and aesthetic quality" in generated images. The concept is thus presented as a multifaceted aspect of generation, covering both artistic and technical characteristics.
