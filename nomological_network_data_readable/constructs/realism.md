# Realism
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided sources, Realism is a construct that characterizes how closely generated artifacts resemble real-world phenomena. The definitions vary by application domain. One framing, specific to autonomous driving, defines it as "the degree to which the generated traffic trajectories and multi-vehicle scenarios resemble the dynamics and behaviors found in the real world" ("Language-Driven Interactive Traffic Trajectory Generation"). A more general definition, applied to synthetic data tables, describes it as "the quality of synthetic data appearing plausible and indistinguishable from real-world data" ("Evaluating Language Translation Models by Playing Telephone"). The field operationalizes and measures this construct in multiple ways. For instance, one paper proposes ensuring realism by imposing technical constraints on generated data, such as "Distributional similarity" and "Logical consistency". Other work indicates that Realism is also measured through "Human evaluation".
