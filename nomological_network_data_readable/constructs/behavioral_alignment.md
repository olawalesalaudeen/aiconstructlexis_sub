# Behavioral alignment
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  
**Also known as:** User behavior alignment  

## State of the Field

The prevailing usage of "Behavioral alignment" in the provided literature refers to the degree of similarity between the behavior of a large language model agent and that of a human. This similarity is conceptualized in terms of the "correspondence of factors impacting the behavior and the dynamics of actions over time," as one paper puts it ("Can Large Language Model Agents Simulate Human Trust Behavior?"). Another study operationalizes this concept by evaluating alignment across specific dimensions such as "linguistic style, emotional expression... and strategic behavior" within complex social interactions. A more specific framing, termed "User behavior alignment," defines the concept as an LLM's ability to model user actions within a particular domain. For instance, in an online shopping context, this is operationalized by the model's capacity to handle "queries, clicks, purchases, and reviews," a skill that one paper notes general LLMs may lack due to their training data ("Shopping MMLU..."). The construct is also reported in one paper to correlate with "Brain alignment," though no further details on this relationship are provided.
