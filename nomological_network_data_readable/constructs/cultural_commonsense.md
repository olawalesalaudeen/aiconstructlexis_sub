# Cultural commonsense
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided data, the construct of 'Cultural commonsense' is defined in one paper as the shared background knowledge about culturally specific social expectations. This knowledge is characterized as being non-obvious to individuals outside a given culture while serving to guide appropriate social interactions within it. The concept is illustrated with the example of 'Taarof' in Persian culture, which is described as a form of cultural commonsense that is "shared within Persian culture but often not intuitive to outsiders" (SwarmAgentic: Towards Fully Automated Agentic System Generation via Swarm Intelligence).
