# Semantic richness
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  
**Also known as:** Information richness  

## State of the Field

Across the provided sources, semantic richness is characterized in two distinct contexts: the completeness of retrieved information and the complexity of generated text. One paper, using the term 'Information richness,' defines the concept as the degree to which retrieved candidates possess 'detailed, semantically complete attributes' necessary for decision-making, such as ranking. This is operationalized by enriching prompts with contextual metadata. A second framing defines 'Semantic richness' in the context of generated captions, referring to their capacity to 'capture complex, diverse, and contextually grounded meanings.' In this usage, the construct is associated with observable text properties like greater length, 'richer vocabulary,' and 'higher readability complexity.' A separate paper also reports that semantic richness influences audio understanding.
