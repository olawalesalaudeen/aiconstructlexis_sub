# Travel planning
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, travel planning is conceptualized in two distinct ways: as a latent cognitive capability and as a concrete generation task. One perspective defines it as the "general latent capability to produce feasible, personalized travel itineraries from user requirements and contextual constraints" (MIRROR: Multimodal Cognitive Reframing Therapy for Rolling with Resistance). This framing emphasizes the underlying "automated travel planning abilities" of a system, with a focus on improving the "ability of real-world travel plan generation." Alternatively, another study frames it as "the task of autonomously generating a travel plan" from a natural language query (Self-Augmented Preference Alignment for Sycophancy Reduction inLLMs). In this view, the output is a "sequence of daily tourist activities and Points of Interest (POIs)," such as a "single-city, multi-day travel plan," with LLMs positioned to serve as travel agents.
