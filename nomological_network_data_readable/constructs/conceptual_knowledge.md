# Conceptual knowledge
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  

## State of the Field

Conceptual knowledge is most commonly defined as a model's internal representation of abstract concepts and their definitions, positioned as distinct from simple factual knowledge. One paper elaborates on this distinction, noting that "conceptual knowledge is more abstract and more difficult to edit than factual knowledge" ("Perturbation-Restrained Sequential Model Editing"). A related framing describes it as a "human-understandable group of features... that can be localized within a model’s internal representations," often in the context of knowledge erasure tasks for concepts like "Harry Potter or Guns." Across the provided sources, this construct is operationalized and measured through specific benchmarks, particularly in studies involving model editing or unlearning. For instance, its manipulation is evaluated using the ConceptEdit dataset, while the ConceptVectors benchmark is used to source concepts for unlearning evaluations. The construct is also studied alongside Conceptualization.
