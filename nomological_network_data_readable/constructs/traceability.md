# Traceability
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, Traceability is consistently defined as the degree to which a model's outputs can be linked back to their origins, primarily for user verification. The concept is framed both broadly and in specific applications. One general definition describes it as linking outputs to "specific sources or reasoning steps" to allow for "verification and error analysis" ("DeepSolution..."). A more specific use case appears in summarization, where it is defined as grounding a summary in "source sentences that users can inspect," often through mechanisms like "sentence-level citations" ("EMNLP..."). The stated purpose of traceability is to enable users to "assess their accuracy" and to enhance interpretability. Conversely, a lack of traceability, resulting from "opaque generation processes," is presented as a challenge that hinders error analysis and raises accountability issues ("DeepSolution...").
