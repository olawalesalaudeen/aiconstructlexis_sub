# Evidence grounding
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided literature, "Evidence grounding" is defined as a model's ability to support its responses with specific, relevant, and accurate references to observable content from the input, such as visual scenes, rather than making unsupported claims. This construct is operationalized by evaluating the "supportiveness" of a model's claims, which is described as "the degree to which they are grounded on the provided reference context." The evaluation also considers the "quality and relevance of the evidence provided." One paper notes that improving this capability enhances responses with "more precise scene descriptions and behavioral insights." The same work also states that this ability allows a model to "reference specific scenes and provide concrete examples to support its assertions."
