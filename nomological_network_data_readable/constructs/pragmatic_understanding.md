# Pragmatic understanding
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  
**Also known as:** Pragmatic comprehension, Empathic understanding  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, pragmatic understanding is most commonly framed as the ability to interpret meaning beyond literal expressions by leveraging context. The prevailing definitions describe this as an ability to understand how context shapes meaning, which includes interpreting speaker intent, cultural knowledge, non-literal expressions, and implicatures. One paper describes this as a task that requires "moving beyond surface-level coherence to grasp complex rhetorical intent" ("SPARK: Simulating the Co-evolution of Stance and Topic Dynamics in Online Discourse withLLM-based Agents"), noting that such understanding is relevant for developing "socially intelligent systems." Another source highlights that this is studied because "intended meanings often diverge from literal expressions (i.e., implicature)" ("Improving Large Language Models Function Calling and Interpretability via Guided-Structured Templates"). A more specialized conceptualization, termed "empathic understanding," is also present. This variant is defined not as general non-literal interpretation, but as the ability to "convey a deep understanding of the user’s inner emotional world" by "reflecting feelings and aligning with the client’s subjective experience" ("VisEscape: A Benchmark for Evaluating Exploration-driven Decision-making in Virtual Escape Rooms").
