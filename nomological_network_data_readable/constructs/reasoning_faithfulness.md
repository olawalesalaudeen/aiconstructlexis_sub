# Reasoning faithfulness
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, reasoning faithfulness is a construct with several distinct definitions. The most prevalent framing, appearing in multiple papers, defines it as the degree to which a model's generated reasoning path accurately reflects the actual process leading to its answer, avoiding misleading justifications. A different perspective, found in one paper, focuses on internal consistency, where unfaithfulness occurs when 'derived conclusions do not adhere to the previously generated reasoning chain' ("Self-calibration for Language Model Quantization and Pruning"). A third definition characterizes it as the extent to which a reasoning trace accurately reflects the model’s 'internal decision-making process' ("DSCD: Large Language Model Detoxification with Self-Constrained Decoding"). This construct is associated with generating high-quality outputs, with one study aiming to select 'faithful and high-quality Chains of Thought (CoTs)' to enhance the reliability of model reasoning ("Unfamiliar Finetuning Examples Control How Language Models Hallucinate").
