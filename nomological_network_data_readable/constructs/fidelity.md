# Fidelity
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  
**Also known as:** Image fidelity  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, Fidelity is predominantly defined in the context of generative models, where it refers to the quality of generated outputs. This common usage characterizes Fidelity as the degree to which outputs are "realistic, high-quality, and free from artifacts," with one paper specifying this as "Image fidelity" to describe the absence of "unintended distortions." In this context, it is discussed as a desirable property of generation systems, with one study noting its method "generates images with greater fidelity." A distinct and less frequent usage appears in the context of model interpretability, where Fidelity is framed differently. In this alternative view, from "Sparse Autoencoders for Hypothesis Generation," the construct is defined as "the accuracy with which a natural language concept captures the activation pattern of a neuron." This paper provides a specific operationalization, measuring the "fidelity of the interpretation" using a formula based on precision and recall.
