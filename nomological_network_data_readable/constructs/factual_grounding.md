# Factual grounding
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided data from a single paper, factual grounding is a construct defined as the degree to which a model's outputs align with real-world distributions and factual information. The source presents it as a goal of model alignment, seeking to "guide models toward producing outputs grounded in factual information and real-world context." This construct is operationalized conceptually through the objective of aligning a model with a "real-world" distribution. This approach is explicitly contrasted with aligning to an "equal" distribution, which the paper associates with social equity. This contrast highlights a stated relationship between factual grounding and the concept of fairness.
