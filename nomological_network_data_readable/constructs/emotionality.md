# Emotionality
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided data, Emotionality is characterized within a single paper as a latent construct in large language models. It is defined as the model's tendency to express emotional sensitivity and vulnerability. The primary effect associated with this construct is its reported influence on the sentiment of generated text. The source material describes a non-linear relationship where both high and low levels of Emotionality are linked to an increase in negative sentiment. As the paper notes, "it is evident that both high and low scores lead to an increase in negative responses in text generation tasks" (Multimedia Event Extraction withLLMKnowledge Editing). The provided data does not specify how this construct is measured, only its observed impact on text generation outcomes.
