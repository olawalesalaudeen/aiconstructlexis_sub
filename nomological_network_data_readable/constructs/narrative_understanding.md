# Narrative understanding
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

In the provided literature, narrative understanding is defined as a latent ability to capture and interpret the key details and implications of a story. This construct is operationalized through the behavior of summarization, which is explicitly framed as a means of assessment. As one study notes, "summarization is a vehicle for examining the understanding the summarizer has of the story" ("Comparing Specialised Small and General Large Language Models on Text Classification: 100 Labelled Samples to Achieve Break-Even Performance"). This approach suggests that the act of producing or evaluating a summary, which involves "capturing and distilling the key details and takeways from that narrative," serves as the observable evidence for this underlying ability. Additionally, the construct is noted in one paper as being related to language proficiency.
