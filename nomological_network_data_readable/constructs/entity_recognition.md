# Entity recognition
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

The construct of entity recognition is framed in at least two distinct ways across the provided literature. One paper defines it as a model's latent, internal capacity to distinguish between entities it recognizes and those it does not, specifically by detecting if it can recall facts about them. This capacity is investigated by analyzing a model's "internal representations at the entity token" to discover "directions in the representation space that suggest the presence of encoded knowledge awareness." In contrast, another paper presents entity recognition as a multimodal task involving the identification of "culturally significant entities, such as people, places, or artifacts, based on visual and linguistic cues." In this context, datasets like MERLIN and M3LS are cited as resources used to "improve entity recognition." Thus, the term is applied to both a model's internal self-awareness regarding its knowledge from text and a behavior involving identification from images and text.
