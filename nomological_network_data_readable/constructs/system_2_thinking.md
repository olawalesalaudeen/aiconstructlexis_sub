# System 2 thinking
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 8 papers  
**Also known as:** System-2 thinking, System 2 reasoning, Deliberate reasoning  

## State of the Field

Across the provided papers, System 2 thinking is consistently described as a cognitive mode involving slow, deliberate, and analytical processing, analogous to human cognition. The definitions commonly highlight its logical, methodical, and reflective nature, contrasting it with faster, more "unconscious" styles of answering ("Chain of Preference Optimization"). Its function is frequently tied to complex problem-solving, with a recurring emphasis on its role in deep error detection through the "careful examination of assumptions, conditions, calculations, and logic" ("MR-GSM8K"). A less common framing defines it as a "latent tendency to engage in deliberate, reflective reasoning that can revise and improve an initial output" ("ChartMimic"). The construct is operationalized in several ways: as the capacity for self-reflection, which is described as a manifestation of this reasoning; the generation of intermediate steps like a "search trace" ("Dualformer"); and the exploration of multiple reasoning paths. Additionally, the ability to "reason about reasoning" is presented as a meta-evaluative tool for its assessment ("MR-GSM8K"), while another view suggests it manifests as consistent, rule-based processing that is "not swayed by incidental or superficial features" ("SETLEXSEM CHALLENGE"). System 2 thinking is studied alongside safety and evaluation, and is reported in one paper to influence hierarchical reasoning. The surveyed work often frames this capability as underdeveloped in current models.
