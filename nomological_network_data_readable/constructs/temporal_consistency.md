# Temporal consistency
**Type:** Construct  
**Referenced in:** 7 papers  
**Also known as:** Temporal coherence  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, 'Temporal consistency', often used interchangeably with 'Temporal coherence', is predominantly discussed in the context of video generation and analysis. The most common framing defines it as the quality of a video maintaining a smooth and logically continuous progression of events and objects across frames, without flicker or discontinuities. A more specific application within this domain is in video segmentation, where the construct refers to producing object segmentation masks that are stable and coherent across consecutive frames. A distinct usage appears in the context of text, where one paper defines 'Temporal coherence' as the 'logical and chronological flow of ideas across sentences'. The construct is operationalized and assessed through various metrics depending on the modality. For video, papers mention the use of CHScore to evaluate 'logical progression and continuity' and Flow-score (FScore). In the domain of text, the BBScoreV2 is noted for its ability to 'capture temporal structure' and 'assess document coherence'.
