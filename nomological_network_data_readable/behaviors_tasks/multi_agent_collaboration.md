# Multi-agent collaboration
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, multi-agent collaboration is consistently described as a behavior where multiple agents work together to achieve a collective goal. The prevailing framing characterizes this process as one of "collective deliberation through cross-examination and debate," as noted in "MatViX: Multimodal Information Extraction from Visually Rich Articles." A different perspective, offered in "FLEURS-ASL," defines it as the "observable coordination and joint output generation among multiple specialized agents" intended to "synergize their specialized expertise." Yet another view, from "Graph World Model," focuses on generating task-oriented outputs through interaction between agents and with external knowledge sources. The stated purpose of this collaborative behavior is often to improve model performance and reveal emergent abilities. The provided data offers limited detail on measurement, though one paper operationalizes the behavior within the task of answering medical questions, and it is reported to be a factor in evaluation.
