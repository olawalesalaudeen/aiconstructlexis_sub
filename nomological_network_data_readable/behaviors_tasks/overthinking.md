# Overthinking
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 9 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, Overthinking is most commonly characterized as a model's tendency to generate inefficient, verbose, and redundant reasoning processes, particularly for simple problems. This dominant framing, found in multiple papers, describes the behavior as producing "excessive reasoning steps" or "redundant solutions" that lead to high computational cost with minimal benefit to accuracy. One paper specifies this as the "inefficient use of generated tokens." A different framing is presented in one study, which defines Overthinking in an interactive context as a model asking "overly detailed or redundant clarification questions that deviate from the original query’s intent." This behavior is operationalized by observing superfluous steps in a model's process, with one paper noting its analysis on mathematical benchmarks. One study reports that these overthinking patterns are associated with a lack of "diversity in reasoning strategies." The concept is also studied in relation to repetitive generation.
