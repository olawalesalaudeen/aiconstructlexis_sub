# Embodied question answering
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the provided sources, Embodied Question Answering (EQA) is defined as a task where an agent answers questions about a 3D environment. The behavior is operationalized by requiring an agent to perform a sequence of actions, including perceiving, navigating, and interacting with its surroundings based on multimodal observations like video frames. One paper notes that the agent "must actively explore its environment to answer questions posed in natural language" ("From Tens of Hours to Tens of Thousands: Scaling Back-Translation for Speech Recognition"). EQA is consistently framed as a complex task that involves multiple underlying abilities. The provided literature connects it to spatial understanding, temporal understanding, and logical reasoning. One source states that evaluating EQA allows for a "nuanced evaluation of agents’ spatial, temporal, and logical understanding" ("Parallel Continuous Chain-of-Thought with Jacobi Iteration"). The connection to reasoning is further specified, as the task "requires agents to... reason through sequences of multimodal observations and actions" to succeed.
