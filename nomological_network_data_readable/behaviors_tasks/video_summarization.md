# Video summarization
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, video summarization is characterized as a behavior focused on producing summaries of video content by processing evidence from individual frames. The definitions are largely in agreement, though one offers a more specific operationalization involving the aggregation of frame descriptions using Chain-of-Thought and in-context learning. This method is implemented via a "summary agent" that takes frame descriptions as context to produce a summary. Another paper discusses the task more generally as selecting evidence from frames, but cautions that simply "matching frames might overlook the subtle transitions that connect scenes" ("Frame-Voyager: Learning to Query Frames for Video Large Language Models"). Video summarization is presented as a "typical video-language task" alongside video understanding and reasoning, and is also reported to be related to video question answering, with one paper stating it can be "formulated as" that task.
