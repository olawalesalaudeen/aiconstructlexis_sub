# Multi-agent debate
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 9 papers  
**Also known as:** Multi-round discussion, Multi-turn debate  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, multi-agent debate is consistently described as an interactive, multi-turn process involving multiple agents collaborating to refine an outcome. The terms 'multi-round discussion' and 'multi-turn debate' are also used to refer to this behavior. The common goal is for agents to exchange information, reasoning steps, or critiques to reach a consensus, refine conclusions, or improve system designs. One definition specifies that agents may begin with "differing initial viewpoints" and engage in "reflective discourse, critically revising their stances through mutual exchange" (TS-CLIP: Time Series Understanding byCLIP). As a research task, this behavior is operationalized in the context of logical reasoning. For example, one paper notes the practice of building "multi-agent debate tasks on several mathematical or logical reasoning datasets" (PRISM: Efficient Long-Range Reasoning With Short-ContextLLMs). Multi-agent debate is also studied in relation to self-consistency.
