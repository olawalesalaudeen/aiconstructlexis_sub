# News article generation
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

In the provided literature, news article generation is defined as the behavior of producing full-length news articles from language models. The task is operationalized in at least two ways: by prompting models to "write articles referencing the provided Public Opinion Set" or, in other cases, to "generate a news article given a headline." The resulting articles are expected to include elements such as "headlines, quotations, and narrative framing." A recurring application of this task is to investigate model alignment, with one definition explicitly framing the behavior as a way to test for `Political bias` and neutrality. This focus is further evidenced by research titled "Fair or Framed? Political Bias in News Articles Generated by LLMs." The behavior is also studied within the context of the `OpenTuringBench` benchmark. Analysis of this behavior can occur at a large scale, as one study notes, "we analyze 10,850 articles."
