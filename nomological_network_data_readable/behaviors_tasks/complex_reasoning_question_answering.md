# Complex reasoning question answering
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  
**Also known as:** Reasoning question answering, Complex question answering  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, complex reasoning question answering is consistently described as a behavior requiring cognitive operations that extend beyond simple recognition, such as inference, reasoning, or the synthesis of multiple pieces of information. While this core idea is shared, the specific context in which the behavior is studied varies. One paper defines it in relation to understanding visual scenes from fMRI data, another focuses on synthesizing information from charts to perform comparisons or analysis, and a third presents it more generally for multimodal models. This behavior is operationalized as a distinct task category for evaluation, with one paper noting its inclusion in the LLaVA-Bench benchmark. The difficulty of this task is highlighted in one study, which states that "All models struggle with reasoning questions" in the context of chart understanding.
