# Cultural text adaptation
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  
**Also known as:** Cultural context adaptation  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, cultural text adaptation is characterized as the behavior of modifying a text to align with a different cultural context. One paper frames this as "cultural context adaptation," defining it as the task of recontextualizing educational materials to reflect the "cultural and social norms of different regions." A second paper offers a more detailed process, describing it as transforming a text from a source to a target cultural context by "replacing culturally grounded entities while preserving semantic intent." This work further specifies that the goal is to align the text with the target group’s “linguistic, social, and conceptual norms.” The provided data does not contain information on how this behavior is operationalized or measured.
