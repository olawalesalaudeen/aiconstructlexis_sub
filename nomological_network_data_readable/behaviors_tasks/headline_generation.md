# Headline generation
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

The provided material characterizes headline generation as the observable task of producing a concise and relevant title for a given body of text. This behavior is framed as a supervised task, with one study noting a lack of relevant corpora for it. To operationalize this task, models are fine-tuned on specialized datasets, such as the CMHG dataset which was designed for Tibetan, Uyghur, and Mongolian. Performance is then evaluated using high-quality annotated data. The ROUGE-L score is used as a metric to assess whether the models can generate "concise and contextually accurate headlines."
