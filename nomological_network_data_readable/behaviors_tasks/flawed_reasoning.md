# Flawed reasoning
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  
**Also known as:** Calculation error, Reasoning Errors, Problem misunderstanding, Making systematic errors  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, flawed reasoning is characterized as an observable failure in a model's problem-solving process. The concept is defined in several distinct ways, encompassing errors in logic, computation, and problem interpretation. One line of work breaks down these failures into specific categories, including 'flawed reasoning' itself (logical fallacies or incorrect steps), 'calculation error' (incorrect numerical computation), and 'problem misunderstanding' (misinterpreting the prompt). In one analysis, calculation errors were found to be a 'major concern' and the 'largest category' of mistakes. Other research defines it as a failure to apply complex reasoning even when sensory input is correctly perceived, with one study noting such errors 'account for a significant portion of mistakes'. A different framing describes it as 'making systematic errors,' which are predictable flaws in the model's reasoning process that can sometimes be explained by probing its internal states. The provided data indicates that this behavior is measured using human evaluation and is studied in relation to commonsense knowledge.
