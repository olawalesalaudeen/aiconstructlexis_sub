# Safety judgment
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  
**Also known as:** Ethical judgment, Moral judgment  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, the behavior of 'Safety judgment' is characterized in several related ways, including as a direct safety assessment, a broader ethical evaluation, or a judgment of moral acceptability. One framing defines it as the task of categorizing a user's query or intent as either 'safe' or 'unsafe' based on context, which can include visual information. This is operationalized by having models perform a binary classification or assign a 'safety score' to a query. A second perspective treats this as an 'Ethical Judgment' task, which involves evaluating text according to ethical principles like deontology and justice. A third framing defines it as 'Moral judgment,' where the task is to decide if a described action, such as 'stealing the bread,' is 'morally acceptable' within a given scenario. In this context, models are evaluated on their capacity to predict human judgments about these moral scenarios. While the specific criteria differ, all three framings conceptualize this behavior as an evaluative classification or prediction task.
