# Plan revision
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  
**Also known as:** Course correction  

## State of the Field

Plan revision, also referred to as "course correction," is a behavior characterized by the modification of an initial plan, preference, or intended action based on new information. The provided literature describes the triggers for this behavior in two distinct contexts. One framing defines it as a response to shifts in "user preferences or environmental conditions such as weather or transportation disruptions" within tasks like travel planning, incorporating diverse cases related to dining, budget, or activities. A second framing treats it as an agent's adjustment after "hearing other agents or receiving new information," where an agent might alter a planned action based on another agent's commitments or new details that emerge during a dialogue. Across these uses, the core idea is an agent's capacity to dynamically adjust its course based on updated inputs. The provided data describes the scenarios where this behavior is observed but does not specify any benchmarks or evaluation procedures used to measure it.
