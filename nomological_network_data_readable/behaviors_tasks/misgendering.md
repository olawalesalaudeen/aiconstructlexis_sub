# Misgendering
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the available data, misgendering is characterized as a behavior where a model's completion incorrectly assigns a gender to a subject that differs from the one specified in the prompt. The provided definition and source snippets frame this as a directional phenomenon, specifically the assignment of masculine forms to feminine or neutral inputs. This behavior is operationalized by comparing the gender in a prompt to the gender in the model's generated text. One study reports that while "no instances of misgendering masculine prompts occurred," misgendering was observed for feminine or neutral prompts. The same work further specifies that "the large majority of all misgendered identities are interpreted as masculine," reinforcing the observation of a default-to-masculine pattern.
