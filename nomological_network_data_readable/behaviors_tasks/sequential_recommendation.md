# Sequential recommendation
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Based on the available literature, sequential recommendation is consistently framed as the task of predicting a user's next interaction based on their chronologically ordered history of previous interactions. The objective is described as predicting "users’ subsequent choices based on their historical interactions" or the "next likely item for users based on their historical records" (LLM-ESR: Large Language Models Enhancement for Long-tailed Sequential Recommendation). One paper formalizes this problem as maximizing the probability of the next item given the user's sequence of past items. To operationalize and evaluate this predictive behavior, the field uses specific datasets. The provided sources indicate that sequential recommendation is measured using datasets such as LastFM and MovieLens.
