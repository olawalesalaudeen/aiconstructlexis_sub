# Professional question answering
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 3 papers  

## State of the Field

Professional question answering is characterized as the behavior of generating responses to queries that demand domain-specific expertise. The provided source material defines this expertise as spanning fields like medicine, law, finance, or music, with the objective of producing information that is both accurate and contextually appropriate. This behavior is elicited through what one paper calls "professional questions" or "role-specific professional queries." Performance on this task is used to evaluate and compare model capabilities. For instance, one study contrasts a model that "tends to avoid answering questions directly" with a method that can "more accurately demonstrate the professional capabilities of lawyers" (Large Language Models Have Intrinsic Meta-Cognition, but Need a Good Lens).
