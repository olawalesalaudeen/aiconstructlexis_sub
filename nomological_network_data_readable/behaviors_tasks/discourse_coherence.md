# Discourse coherence
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 6 papers  
**Also known as:** Coherent response generation, Incoherent generation, Coherent text generation  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, discourse coherence is predominantly framed as an observable behavior where a model produces text that is logical, consistent, and easy to understand. This is sometimes specified as maintaining "logical and thematic consistency across sentences or paragraphs" ("Sketch-of-Thought: EfficientLLMReasoning with Adaptive Cognitive-Inspired Sketching"). A less common framing defines its absence, "incoherent generation," as an "undesirable output pattern" that can result from over-correction and may co-occur with evasiveness or repetition, as one study notes ("Less is More: The Effectiveness of Compact Typological Language Representations"). Several papers suggest that this capability can be improved through methods like instruction-tuning. The provided data offers limited detail on how this behavior is measured, with one paper mentioning the use of "Win-rates to gauge coherent language generation capabilities" ("JUREX-4E: Juridical Expert-Annotated Four-Element Knowledge Base for Legal Reasoning").
