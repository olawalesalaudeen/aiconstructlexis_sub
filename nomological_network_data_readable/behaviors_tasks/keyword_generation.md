# Keyword generation
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  
**Also known as:** Ad keyword generation  

## State of the Field

Keyword generation is a behavior framed in several distinct ways across the provided literature. One common application is producing a short list of words or phrases to characterize a person, with prompts asking for keywords that describe their "characteristics" or "personality traits" (DreamSync: Aligning Text-to-Image Generation with Image Understanding Feedback). Another prevalent line of work treats this behavior in the context of online advertising, defining it as "Ad keyword generation" for sponsored search campaigns based on product descriptions. This process is noted as being "fully automated with minimal human supervision" in some frameworks (FacLens: Transferable Probe for Foreseeing Non-Factuality in Fact-Seeking Question Answering of Large Language Models). A different conceptualization focuses on summarizing documents, where the task is to produce "salient keywords or terms that represent the core concepts of a document." One study notes this can include generating "terms not present in the document itself" (AdvisorQA: Towards Helpful and Harmless Advice-seeking Question Answering with Collective Intelligence). Across these applications, the behavior is operationalized by prompting large language models, sometimes guided by few-shot demonstrations.
