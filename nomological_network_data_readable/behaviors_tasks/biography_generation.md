# Biography generation
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 5 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the surveyed literature, biography generation is consistently defined as the task of producing a biographical passage or description about a person or entity from a prompt. The behavior is typically elicited using direct questions, with examples like "Tell me a bio of Taral Hicks" and "Tell me a bio of <entity>" appearing in the source material. One paper specifies that this task draws on a model's "general world knowledge" to produce the description. To operationalize the task, one study reports using entities from the FactScore dataset. The behavior is also noted as being related to the concept of 'Biography'.
