# Multimodal dialogue
**Type:** Behavior  
**Referenced in:** 4 papers  

## State of the Field

Across the provided literature, multimodal dialogue is consistently defined as a conversational exchange where the context involves multiple modalities, most commonly text and images. This behavior requires a model to both understand and generate content across these formats. A more specific framing operationalizes this as a task-oriented dialogue requiring step-by-step reasoning and decision-making. In this instantiation, the model references multiple interleaved images to complete a goal, such as to "place a soap container on a counter," prompted by conversational turns like "What’s the next step?" ("Fine-tuning Multimodal LLMs to Follow Zero-shot Demonstrative Instructions"). Multimodal dialogue is positioned as a task where Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) are reported to excel, alongside visual question answering and grounded generation. The behavior is subject to evaluation, with one paper referencing its "qualitative results and comparisons" ("DreamLLM: Synergistic Multimodal Comprehension and Creation").
